- [Liquid Nitrogen herb preservation] — Blanch herbs before flash-freezing in liquid nitrogen to prevent cell rupture, blackening, and the development of off-aromas.
- [Aqueous Ozone] — Wash aromatics in this solution to prevent rot and maintain freshness.
- [N2O (Nitrous Oxide)] — Use as a beverage balancing agent to increase "liveliness" and add sweetness without the sourness associated with CO2.
- [N2 (Nitrogen)] — Use for beverage dispensing to provide pressure and create creamy textures without significantly increasing carbonation.
- [Soft water for kombu] — Use soft or bottled water when making delicate kombu stocks to prevent tap water minerals from negatively impacting the flavor.
- [Low-temperature fat rendering] — Pre-sear meat before a low-temperature cook to avoid a "hammy" texture in the rendered fat.
- [Sous vide bacterial mitigation] — Dunk bags in simmering water before cooking to kill surface lactic acid bacteria that produce sauerkraut or blue cheese odors.
- [Stabilized cocktail foams] — Use lecithin (0.5–0.75%) or xanthan gum (max 0.25%) and an angled stick blender to create stable, aerated foams.
- [Salap (Orchid Powder) ice cream] — Add at 0.5–1% of total weight and avoid using a Pacojet to prevent the machine from chopping the delicate, stretchy, gluten-like network formed during manual beating.
- [Sous vide rib texture progression] — Cook at 60°C (140°F) for 24 hours to achieve skirt-steak texture or 24–30 hours for a short-rib texture.
