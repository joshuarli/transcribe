[Cocktail Equilibrium] — Achieve desired chilling and dilution by shaking for 12–15 seconds or stirring for over 60 seconds, relying on the melting of ice rather than temperature differentials to drive the process.

[Warmed-over flavor (WOF)] — Use vacuum bagging to mitigate the oxidation of polyunsaturated fats, which causes meat to develop "cardboardy" off-flavors during chilling and reheating.

[Flavor Leveling] — Allow high-note volatile compounds in heavy sauces to hydrolyze and degrade during the cooling and reheating cycle to create a more integrated, "evened out" flavor profile.

[Liquid Nitrogen Pre-freezing] — Suspend food above liquid nitrogen for several minutes before immersion to prevent hyper-brittleness during the rapid freezing process.

[Salted Ice Slush] — Mix ice with 25% salt by weight to create a rapid-freezing medium that produces small, high-quality ice crystals.

[Running Water Thawing] — Submerge bagged food in running water to overcome the insulating effect of the water layer that forms on the food surface as it thaws.

[Freeze-Thaw Stabilizers] — Utilize Locust Bean Gum, flavor-free Guar Gum (such as TIC gums), or Xanthan Gum to prevent weeping in sauces subjected to freezing, as gelatin and agar are unstable in freeze-thaw cycles.

[Rotovap Capsaicin Extraction] — Use a Rotovap at approximately 40°C to remove capsaicin from peppers while preserving delicate floral aromas that would be lost in higher-temperature stovetop distillation.

[Pressure Cooker Farro Method] — Cook farro in a pressure cooker for 30–40 minutes with excess liquid to force moisture through the tough seed coats.

[Exotic Meat Temperature Parameters] — Manage protein toughness by cooking lion at 60°C for 10 hours, raccoon at 63°C for 24 hours (then shredding), beaver flapper at 80°C for several hours, or yak at 60°C for 24 hours to achieve a skirt-steak texture.
