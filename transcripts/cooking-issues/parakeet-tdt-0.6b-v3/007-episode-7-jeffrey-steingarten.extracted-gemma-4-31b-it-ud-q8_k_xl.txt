### Techniques & Parameters

**Soufflé Stabilization & Pre-making**
*   **iSi Whipper:** Pure egg white foam does not work directly in an iSi whipper.
*   **Stabilization:** To pre-make soufflés, use stabilizers like **carrageenan** or **xanthan gum**.
*   **Process:** Create a stabilized foam, cut into "pucks," and bake. 
*   **Physics:** Soufflés rely on a pre-aerated base that inflates via steam and bubble expansion. Use stabilizers that melt above $120^\circ\text{F}$ to $140^\circ\text{F}$ to allow expansion during heating.

**Avocado Oxidation Prevention**
*   **Ineffective:** Placing the pit in the puree.
*   **Effective:** 
    *   **Ascorbic Acid (Vitamin C):** Pure powder is recommended over citrus. It provides high antioxidant power with less acidity/taste.
    *   **Citrus:** Lime juice works but is often too acidic.
    *   **Chemicals:** Sodium metabisulfite.
    *   **Physical:** Using oxygen-proof vacuum bags (standard plastic wrap is insufficient).

**Clam & Snail Purging**
*   **Clams:** Cornmeal is used to stimulate eating and purging (likely via starch dissolution).
*   **Snails (Spanish Style):** Purge on herbs (e.g., rosemary) to infuse the meat with flavor.
*   **Snails (Roman Style):** Fatten on milk.

**Reducing Heat in Peppers**
*   **Manual:** Carefully remove the inner skin (where most capsaicin is concentrated) using a knife.
*   **Enzymatic:** Peelzyme (SPL) is ineffective for targeted heat removal because it dissolves the mesocarp (the "meat" of the pepper).
*   **Alternative:** Use *Aji dulce* peppers (Venezuelan); they share the floral aroma of habaneros but are sweet/mild.

### Ingredients & Functional Roles

*   **Transglutaminase ("Meat Glue"):**
    *   **Proteins:** Glues animal proteins together.
    *   **Dairy/Soy:** Can strengthen tofu, yogurt, and other dairy systems.
    *   **Vegetables:** Cannot glue vegetables alone; requires a "helper protein" like gelatin to cross-link.
*   **MSG (Monosodium Glutamate):**
    *   **Role:** Flavor enhancer (glutamic acid).
    *   **Natural Sources:** Giant kelp.
*   **Horse Fat:** Highly recommended for frying the "best" French fries.

### Caller Q&A

**Q: Should oysters be avoided in months without an "R"?**
**A:** The rule exists because oysters from warm water are less desirable and potentially riskier. Gulf oysters, specifically, are higher risk for *Vibrio* and are often treated with ultra-high pressure pasteurization.

**Q: Does white wine always go with fish?**
**A:** No. Red wine can pair well with fish. White wine is generally too "soft" or weak for steak.

**Q: How do you develop a professional palate?**
**A:** Eat everything. Eat with natives/experts in foreign countries to understand the "correct" version of a dish. To become a "perfect omnivore," intentionally eat foods you dislike to remove personal biases and preferences.

**Q: Is MSG dangerous or does it cause "Chinese Restaurant Syndrome"?**
**A:** Scientifically, MSG is generally safe. Glutamic acid is a natural neurotransmitter. Blood spikes only occur when consumed as a liquid on an empty stomach in very large doses. Reactions in studies are often not statistically significant when double-blinded.
