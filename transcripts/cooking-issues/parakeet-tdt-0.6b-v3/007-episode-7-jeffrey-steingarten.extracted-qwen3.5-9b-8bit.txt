# Techniques & Parameters

*   **Oyster Safety:**
    *   Avoid oysters from warm water (specifically Gulf oysters) due to *Vibrio* risk.
    *   **Pasteurization Method:** Ultra-high pressure pasteurization (UHP) is used on Gulf oysters to kill bacteria.
    *   **Note:** The host has never tasted UHP-treated oysters.
*   **Soufflé Stabilization (Pre-made):**
    *   **Problem:** Soufflés must be started and finished at service; pre-making is difficult.
    *   **Failed Attempt:** Using an ISI Thermo Whip with 4 egg whites and 2 nitrous oxide containers resulted in no foam.
    *   **Solution:** Use a stabilizer.
    *   **Ingredients:** Carrageenan or Xanthan gum.
    *   **Process:** Make base traditionally, stabilize, cut into pucks, bake.
    *   **Mechanism:** The stabilizer sets the initial base but releases/melts when heated (above ~102°C / 215°F), allowing expansion via steam and bubble expansion.
    *   **Commercial Example:** Cuisine Solutions has a commercial preparation based on carrageenan and Xanthan gum.
*   **Clam Purging:**
    *   **Method:** Feed clams a soup with the same salinity as the ocean (e.g., bacon and onion soup).
    *   **Outcome:** Clams eat the soup and absorb the flavor.
    *   **Cornmeal Role:** Presumed to dissolve starch to aid eating, while undissolved parts sink; specific studies cited as unavailable.
*   **Snail Purging/Fattening:**
    *   **Rosemary Method:** Feed snails rosemary for a long period (classic Spanish style).
    *   **Milk Method:** Fatten snails on milk (old Roman technique).
    *   **Success Rate:** Host and friend Johnny Azzini had only marginal success with both methods.
    *   **Alternative:** Feed snails in the garden (host's experience in San Diego).
    *   **Safety Check:** Research genus for poison before eating wild snails.
*   **Avocado Oxidation Prevention:**
    *   **Pit Myth:** Putting the pit in puree does not work.
    *   **Citrus:** Lime juice (3/4 lime to 1 avocado) is common but can be too acidic.
    *   **Ascorbic Acid (Vitamin C):** More effective antioxidant than citrus; less acidic taste.
        *   **Source:** Buy straight 100% Vitamin C powder (avoid pills with fillers like calcium/chalk).
        *   **Mechanism:** Ascorbic acid stops browning; pH level is not the primary preservative.
    *   **Sodium Metabisulfite:** Also works as an antioxidant.
    *   **Packaging:** Oxygen-proof bags are better than standard plastic wrap.
*   **Pepper Heat Reduction (Habaneros):**
    *   **Goal:** Remove capsaicin to taste floral aroma.
    *   **Surgical Method:** Cutting out the inner skin/veins (painful, high risk of contamination).
    *   **Enzymatic Method:** Using SPL (Peelzyme) to melt the inner skin.
        *   **Issue:** The mesocarp (meat) is dissolved by the enzyme; contamination ruins the whole pepper.
    *   **Alternative:** Use *aji dulce* (Venezuelan/Peruvian/Ecuadorian pepper).
        *   **Profile:** Looks like habanero, similar aroma/taste, requires no manipulation.
*   **MSG Usage:**
    *   **Quantity:** Do not use in quantities comparable to salt or spices.
    *   **Application:** Avoid using in soup (too easy to taste/sense). Good for other dishes.
    *   **Natural Source:** Glutamic acid is drained from giant kelp (traditional Asian method).
    *   **Safety:**
        *   Body produces more glutamic acid (neurotransmitter) than can be consumed via MSG.
        *   "Chinese Restaurant Syndrome" (headache/flushing) is largely debunked or linked to unfamiliar food psychology.
        *   **Contaminant Theory:** Historical reactions may be due to impurities from microbial fermentation (purification techniques have improved since the 1960s/70s).
        *   **Salt Sensitivity:** Studies show salt-sensitive individuals do not react to other sodium compounds (e.g., baking soda) in the same way.
*   **French Fry Preparation:**
    *   **Texture:** Crisp but friable outside; inside like mashed potatoes (not too thick).
    *   **Alternative Texture:** Hollow inside (like a rectangular baton/potato chip).
    *   **Fat:** Horse fat provides the best fries.
        *   **Availability:** Found in Paris supermarkets packaged like butter.
        *   **Legal Note:** Eating horse meat is illegal in the US.

# Equipment

*   **Blenders:**
    *   **Vita-Prep:** Excellent for blending/smoothies. High tip speed.
    *   **Waring Commercial:** Comparable or slightly more power than Vita-Prep; pulverizes ingredients in seconds.
        *   **Usage Tip:** Hold down the top to prevent spillage.
    *   **KitchenAid:** Not recommended for high-performance blending compared to the above.
    *   **Selection Criteria:**
        1.  **Tip Speed:** Determines force applied to product.
        2.  **Blade Pitch:** Important for some applications.
        3.  **Pitcher Design:** Must ensure good product movement to avoid improper mixing and cavitation (air pockets).
    *   **Price Points:**
        *   Champion Juicer: ~$200 (good for apples/carrots, not wheatgrass/sugarcane).
        *   Vita-Prep: ~$100-$300.
        *   NutriBullet: ~$5000 (described as a "monster" that eats apples instantly).
*   **Juicers:**
    *   **Champion:** Recommended for juicing (apples, carrots).
    *   **Vita-Prep:** Can be used for blending juice, but not a dedicated juicer.
    *   **Neutra:** High-end centrifuge/enzyme juicer (very expensive).
*   **ISI Thermo Whip:**
    *   **Usage:** Nitrous oxide chargers to foam liquids.
    *   **Limitation:** Cannot effectively foam straight egg whites.
*   **Rotary Evaporator:**
    *   **Suggestion:** Mentioned as a potential tool for removing heat/capsaicin from peppers (though host noted he was told not to mention buying one).
*   **Hand Blender:**
    *   **Invention:** Host invented a hand blender with 24,000 RPM tip speed, variable speed trigger, and high power. Comparable to Vita-Prep.

# Ingredients & Functional Roles

*   **Carrageenan & Xanthan Gum:** Stabilizers for foams (soufflés). Melt above ~102°C to allow expansion.
*   **Gelatin:** Helper protein for cross-linking vegetables with transglutaminase (non-vegetarian).
*   **Transglutaminase ("Meat Glue"):**
    *   **Function:** Glues proteins together.
    *   **Applications:** Strengthening tofu, yogurt/dairy systems, creating vegetable noodles (requires gelatin helper).
    *   **Host Opinion:** Never eaten anything made with meat glue that was worth eating. Most common use is creating even cooking portions (e.g., flat iron steak with cartilage removed).
*   **MSG (Monosodium Glutamate):**
    *   **Function:** Flavor enhancer (same as glutamic acid from kelp).
    *   **Properties:** Provides roughly 1/3 the sodium of table salt by weight.
    *   **Production:** Microbial fermentation.
*   **Cornmeal:** Used in clam purging soup (presumed starch dissolution aids consumption).
*   **Horse Fat:** Renders best for French fries.
*   **Aji Dulce:** Pepper variety from Venezuela/Peru/Ecuador. Habanero-like aroma/taste without heat.

**Caller: Kent Kirschenbaum (NYU)**
*   **Question:** MSG reactions (Chinese Restaurant Syndrome) and salt sensitivity studies.
*   **Answer:**
    *   **MSG:** Historical reactions likely due to fermentation impurities (purification improved). Brain damage studies on rodents used absurd doses and were debunked. Natural glutamic acid is the same as MSG; differences lie in contaminants or psychology of unfamiliar foods.
    *   **Salt:** Studies on salt sensitivity often measured weight, not moles. Public health campaigns conflate salt and sodium.
    *   **Intra Study:** Massive worldwide study (1989) showed no correlation between salt intake and blood pressure in normal populations after eliminating extreme low-salt societies (e.g., Yanomami).
    *   **Conclusion:** Much nutrition science is "crap based on other crap."
