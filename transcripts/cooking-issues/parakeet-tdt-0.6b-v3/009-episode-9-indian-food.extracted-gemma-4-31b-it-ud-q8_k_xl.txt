# Culinary Techniques & Science Notes: Cooking Issues

## Infusion Techniques
### Rapid Infusion (iSi Whipper)
*   **Equipment:** Whipped cream maker (iSi siphon) with nitrous oxide ($\text{N}_2\text{O}$) chargers.
*   **Process:** 
    1. Combine liquid (e.g., liquor) and porous flavor agents (herbs like cilantro/Thai basil, ginger, cocoa nibs) in the canister.
    2. Charge with $\text{N}_2\text{O}$.
    3. Swirl/shake for approx. 1 minute.
    4. Release pressure rapidly.
*   **Mechanism:** High pressure forces liquid into the porous structure of the solids; rapid depressurization causes the liquid to "boil" out, extracting flavors instantly.
*   **Application:** Works for both liquid infusions and infusing solids (e.g., mozzarella).

### Fat Washing
*   **Process:** Mix flavored fat (e.g., smoky bacon fat) with liquor $\rightarrow$ chill until fat solidifies $\rightarrow$ remove the solidified fat cap.
*   **Recommendation:** For bacon-infused cocktails, add a pinch of salt or a hint of sugar to brighten the flavor and prevent it from tasting "flat."

## Potato Science & Applications
### Textures
*   **Stretchy Potato Ice Cream:** Blend steamed potatoes into ice cream base and freeze. 
    *   *Theory:* Freezing acts as a dehydration process, withdrawing water from the potato starch complex to create a stretchy/chewy texture.
*   **Chewiness vs. Crispness:** Chewiness in potatoes is typically a result of partial dehydration.

### Microwave Dehydration
*   **Bread Crumbs/Croutons:** Microwave thin slices of bread slowly. 
    *   *Caution:* Once water is evaporated, burnt areas absorb microwaves rapidly, causing scorched spots.
*   **Microwave "Chips":** Thinly slice potatoes $\rightarrow$ microwave in 30-second intervals until dehydrated. (Note: These are dehydrated slices, not true fried chips).

## Indian Cuisine Fundamentals
### General Approach
*   **Workflow:** Start with ghee $\rightarrow$ sauté onions $\rightarrow$ add aromatics (ginger) $\rightarrow$ sauté spices to release aromas $\rightarrow$ add remaining ingredients.
*   **Beginner Advice:** Avoid dishes requiring a **Tandoor** (e.g., naan, tandoori chicken, chicken tikka masala) as they are difficult to replicate authentically at home.
*   **Ingredients:** Use authentic Basmati rice; make homemade spice mixtures (e.g., garam masala) for maximum freshness.

### Navratan Curry (Arnold's Version)
*   **Base Ingredients:** Sautéed onions, coriander, cumin, ginger, hot pepper, cashew nuts, tomato paste, coconut milk, and pineapple.
*   **Process:** Blend the base ingredients into a sauce $\rightarrow$ toss with steamed vegetables.
*   **Vegan Modification:** Substitute butter/ghee with olive oil and use coconut milk.

## Ingredient Properties & Handling
### Herbs (Mint/Basil)
*   **Problem:** Blackening occurs when cell rupture allows enzymes to mix with compounds in the leaf.
*   **Prevention:**
    *   **Knife Work:** Use an extremely sharp, thin knife; avoid mashing or "whacking" the leaves.
    *   **Blanching:** Brief dip in boiling water kills enzymes (Tradeoff: loses "fresh" raw flavor).
    *   **Alcohol:** Cut herbs directly into alcohol (best for cocktails).

### Sugar & Confections
*   **Meringues:** High sugar by weight, but low sugar by volume due to air.
*   **Sugar Substitutes:** To replace sugar's structural role in meringues, use a non-caloric bulking agent (e.g., **"Lightes"**) combined with a high-intensity sweetener.
*   **Honey:** Highly hygroscopic (attracts water). Unsuitable for confections that must remain dry (e.g., chocolate) as it causes weeping.

## Advanced Thermal Physics
### Supercooling & Superheating
*   **Supercooling:** Cooling a liquid below its freezing point without it solidifying. Requires a **nucleation site** (agitation or bubbles) to trigger freezing.
    *   *Example:* Supercooled soda $\rightarrow$ uncapping releases $\text{CO}_2$ (raising the freezing point) and creates bubbles (nucleation sites) $\rightarrow$ instant slush.
*   **Superheating:** Heating a liquid (typically in a microwave) above its boiling point without bubble formation. Adding a solute (like sugar) can trigger a violent, instantaneous boil.
*   **Mpemba Effect:** The phenomenon where hot water can freeze faster than cold water due to altered nucleation sites.

### Solid Nitrogen Snow
*   **Technique:** Place liquid nitrogen in a vacuum machine $\rightarrow$ run vacuum for several minutes.
*   **Mechanism:** Evaporative cooling lowers the liquid nitrogen's temperature further until it forms solid nitrogen snow on the surface.
