# Culinary Notes: Cooking Issues

## Ingredients & Properties
*   **Tannat (Grape Variety):** High concentration of procyanidins (associated with cardiovascular health).
*   **Cocoa Butter:** Deodorized version recommended for home chocolate making.
*   **Lecithin:** Emulsifier for chocolate; use in very small amounts ($<0.5\%$).
*   **Baking Soda (Sodium Bicarbonate):** 
    *   Alkaline agent that tenderizes meat by increasing pH (protein/collagen breakdown).
    *   When heated, decomposes into $\text{CO}_2$, creating a porous meat structure.
    *   Accelerates browning (Maillard reaction). 
    *   *Warning:* Excessive amounts produce an unpleasant taste.
*   **Egg Whites:** Naturally alkaline (especially as they age/unrefrigerated); contributes to meat tenderization and protects meat from moisture loss during cooking.
*   **Salt (in meat):** Increases water-holding capacity of proteins.

## Vegetable Techniques
### Kale
*   **Preparation:** Chop, remove only the end of the stem (retain center vein).
*   **Cooking:** Sauté onion $\rightarrow$ add garlic at the last minute $\rightarrow$ add kale (it provides its own moisture). 
*   **Finishing:** Add salt and an acid (lemon/vinegar) at the end to brighten flavor.
*   **Recommendation:** Avoid eating kale raw.

### Bitter Greens (Escarole)
*   **Preparation:** Sauté onions and garlic $\rightarrow$ add hot sausage and bacon $\rightarrow$ add escarole.
*   **Finishing:** Add acidity (mustard, vinegar, or lemon) and finish with white beans.

## Chocolate Making
*   **Goal:** Reduce particle size to $\approx 20$ microns for smoothness.
*   **Ingredients:** Roasted cocoa nibs, deodorized cocoa butter, sugar, lecithin, and vanilla (beans or vanilla sugar). 
    *   *Critical:* Avoid water, honey, or vanilla extract; water ruins chocolate.
*   **Equipment:**
    *   **Champion Juicer:** Used for the initial grind of nibs into a paste.
    *   **Santha/Melanger:** Stone wheel refiner used to reach final particle size.
*   **Process:**
    1.  Initial grind in melanger with lid **off** to release volatiles and flash off excess water.
    2.  Cap the machine and run for 2–3 days to achieve smooth texture.
*   **Recommendation:** Quality is dependent on bean selection ("crap in, crap out").

## Meat Techniques
### Velveting
*   **Process:** Marinate small meat chunks in egg white, cornstarch, salt, and occasionally wine or baking soda. 
*   **Setting the Coating:** Pass meat through hot oil or boiling water to set the egg white before stir-frying.
*   **Function:**
    *   **Texture:** The egg/starch coating acts as a barrier, reducing moisture loss.
    *   **Tenderization:** Alkaline egg whites/baking soda break down proteins; salt increases water retention.

## Cocktails & Mixology
### Supercooled Slushies
*   **Concept:** Supercooling a liquid below its freezing point and then initiating rapid nucleation.
*   **Process:**
    1.  Mix alcohol, sugar syrup, and water (omit lime juice/mint as they provide nucleation sites that prevent supercooling).
    2.  Charge in a soda siphon or cream whipper ($\text{N}_2\text{O}$ or $\text{CO}_2$).
    3.  Chill in freezer (approx. $-10^\circ\text{C}$).
    4.  Turn siphon upside down and release gas quickly; the pressure drop and bubble formation trigger crystallization.
    5.  Stir in chilled lime juice at the end.

### Batching & Aeration
*   **Observation:** Shaken drinks are more dilute and texturally different (more aerated) than stirred drinks.
*   **Pre-batching Technique:**
    *   Pre-dilute the cocktail with water.
    *   Store in quart containers in the freezer (do not freeze solid).
    *   **Aeration Trick:** To simulate a fresh shake, shake the chilled, diluted mix in a container containing Hawthorne strainer springs (removed from the strainer).

### Carbonation
*   **Equipment:** 
    *   *Professional/Home:* 20lb $\text{CO}_2$ tank, regulator, and ball fittings (preferred for precision).
    *   *Entry Level:* Cream whipper (preferred over soda siphon).
*   **Siphon/Whipper Technique:**
    1.  **Charge 1:** Shake and vent immediately to remove air/oxygen and nucleation sites.
    2.  **Charge 2:** Ensure liquid is ice cold (add a few ice cubes); charge, shake, and let settle before pouring.
*   **Key Principles:**
    *   **Air:** Headspace air inhibits carbonation and causes foaming.
    *   **Solubility:** $\text{CO}_2$ is more soluble in alcohol than water. Higher proof drinks require higher $\text{CO}_2$ pressure to achieve the same perceived carbonation.

## Caller Q&A
**Q: Can you make low-sugar meringues?**
**A:** Meringues are low-calorie per volume due to high air content. Potential solution: use high-intensity sweeteners combined with a non-nutritive bulking agent that mimics the functional properties of sugar.

**Q: Why does limoncello turn cloudy when adding warm sugar syrup?**
**A:** This is likely the "Uzo effect," where oils soluble in high-proof alcohol precipitate when diluted. Dave hypothesizes that warm syrup creates smaller, more stable oil droplets (increasing bitterness), whereas cold syrup creates larger droplets that coalesce and settle out over several days, leaving the drink clear.
