*   **Goal:** Reduce caloric density while maintaining volume and sweet sensation.

### Kale Preparation
*   **Prep:** Chop kale. Remove only the end of the stem; do not remove the central vein.
*   **Cooking Method:** Sauté.
    1.  Sauté onion first.
    2.  Add garlic at the last minute.
    3.  Add kale (provides its own moisture as it cooks down).
*   **Seasoning:**
    *   Add sufficient salt.
    *   Add acid (e.g., vinegar, lemon) after cooking to brighten.
    *   Optional: Add sweetener.
*   **Alternative (Hearty Greens):** Cook greens with meat (sausage, bacon), beans (white beans), and acidity (mustard, vinegar, lemon).

### Chocolate Making (From Beans)
*   **Ingredients:**
    *   Cocoa nibs (roasted beans).
    *   Cocoa butter (deodorized recommended).
    *   Sugar.
    *   Powdered lecithin (emulsifier, <0.5%).
    *   Vanilla (use whole beans or vanilla sugar; avoid extracts containing water).
    *   *Note:* Cannot make chocolate from nibs alone; requires added cocoa butter.
*   **Equipment:**
    *   **Champion Juicer:** Used to grind nibs into a paste. Grind until warm and liquefied.
    *   **Santhal (Chocolate Melanger):** Small machine with two stone wheels rolling on a round stone surface.
*   **Process:**
    1.  Grind nibs in Champion Juicer until paste forms and warms.
    2.  Add melted cocoa butter, sugar, lecithin, and vanilla.
    3.  **Volatiles Management:** Run machine with lid off for a short time to release volatiles and flash off extra water.
    4.  **Tempering/Texture:** Cap the machine and run for **23 days** to achieve correct texture and smoothness.
*   **Particle Size:** Target **20 microns** or smaller.
*   **Cost:** Initial investment approx. **$500** (Santhal + Champion Juicer).
*   **Resources:** Website `chocolatealchemy.com`.

### Lemoncello Cloudiness (Uzo Effect)
*   **Phenomenon:** Cloudiness occurs when oils precipitate out of solution due to dilution or temperature drop.
*   **Mechanism:** Oils soluble in high-proof alcohol become insoluble when alcohol level drops or temperature decreases, forming oil droplets.
*   **Hypothesis on Temperature:**
    *   Adding warm syrup may create smaller, more stable oil droplets, potentially retaining more lemon oil and increasing bitterness.
    *   Adding cold syrup may create larger droplets that coalesce and settle out over days, potentially clarifying the drink.
*   **Status:** Unproven; host has not conducted definitive tests.

### Velveting Meat
*   **Ingredients:** Egg white, cornstarch, wine, salt, baking soda (optional).
*   **Process:**
    1.  Marinate small chunks of meat in the mixture.
    2.  Set the coating via hot oil or boiling water.
    3.  Proceed to stir-fry.
*   **Scientific Mechanisms:**
    *   **Moisture Retention:** Egg white coating may protect meat, causing water to evaporate from the egg first rather than the meat.
    *   **Alkaline Tenderization:** Egg whites age to become alkaline (pH up to 9). Alkaline conditions increase protein/collagen breakdown rates. Baking soda also tenderizes via alkalinity and ionic character.
    *   **Porosity:** Baking soda decomposes when heated, forming CO2 gas and creating a porous structure interpreted as tenderness.
    *   **Water Holding Capacity:** Salt increases the amount of water proteins can hold.
    *   **Browning:** Alkaline conditions increase browning rates (e.g., pretzels boiled in lye/baking soda). Rapid browning may signal doneness earlier, preventing overcooking.
    *   **Interaction:** Wine acidity may counteract the tenderizing effects of baking soda/egg white alkalinity.

### Frozen Slushies (Supercooling)
*   **Concept:** Supercooling a liquid below its freezing point without ice nucleation, then triggering crystallization.
*   **Constraints:**
    *   **Nucleation Sites:** Mint leaves (crushed) act as nucleation sites, preventing supercooling.
    *   **Lime Juice:** Causes cloudiness/nucleation; should be added last.
*   **Procedure:**
    1.  Mix drink (water, sugar syrup, alcohol, salt) without lime juice.
    2.  Charge with gas (Nitrous Oxide or CO2) in a cream whipper/siphon. Gas lowers freezing point, allowing deeper supercooling.
    3.  Chill mixture to approx. **-8°C to -10°C**.
    4.  Invert whipper/siphon immediately to release gas (bubbles act as nucleation sites) and foam the drink.
    5.  Pour into cup.
    6.  Stir in lime juice.
*   **Guarantee:** Not guaranteed to work; depends on cleanliness and lack of nucleation sites.

### Pre-Diluted Shaken Cocktails
*   **Problem:** Shaken drinks are more diluted and aerated than stirred drinks. Pre-diluting and chilling loses the texture.
*   **Solutions Tested:**
    *   **Blender:** Failed (bubbles too large).
    *   **Liquid Nitrogen:** Close approximation (adds small bubbles as N2 boils off).
    *   **Freezer + Shaking:** Dilute drink, chill in freezer (do not freeze solid), then shake in a cocktail shaker without ice.
    *   **Freezer + Core Container + Springs:** Shake pre-diluted frozen drink in a core container (takeout container) with Hawthorne strainer springs removed.
*   **Results:**
    *   Shaking in a core container with springs produced the most aeration (some preferred least intense).
    *   Shaking in a quart container or standard shaker produced results very close to fresh shaking.
    *   Density/Intensity preferences varied by taster.
*   **Method:** Store pre-diluted cocktails in core containers in the freezer. Shake vigorously before serving.

### Carbonating Cocktails
*   **Equipment:**
    *   **Preferred:** 20-lb CO2 tank with ball fittings (homebrewer style).
        *   Cost: ~$90 for tank, ~$30 for regulator, ~$0.13/ft for hose. Total system under **$81**.
        *   Benefit: Fine-tuned pressure control (e.g., 30-35 psi vs 40 psi changes flavor profile).
    *   **Alternative:** Cream whipper or Soda Siphon.
        *   Limitation: Fixed increments of CO2 (7-8g cartridges); less fine control.
*   **Technique (Whipper/Siphon):**
    1.  **Vent Air:** Charge with 1st cartridge, shake briefly to displace air/nucleation sites, then vent completely.
    2.  **Carbonate:** Add 2nd cartridge (drink must be ice cold; ice cubes help). Shake for carbonation.
    3.  **Settle:** Let sit for a couple of minutes.
    4.  **Pour:** Slowly vent and pour.
*   **Science:**
    *   CO2 is more soluble in alcohol than water.
    *   **Rule:** Higher alcohol content requires higher pressure to achieve equivalent carbonation effect.
    *   **Air Space:** Enemy of carbonation; must minimize headspace.
*   **Recommendations:**
    *   Avoid carbonating rum drinks (personal preference).
    *   Good candidates: Gin drinks, Negronis.
    *   Avoid: Manhattan-style drinks, bitters (flavor distortion).

# Ingredients & Functional Roles

*   **Tanat (Grape Variety):** High in procyanidins; associated with cardiovascular health benefits in studies.
*   **Cocoa Butter:** Essential for chocolate making; provides fat to coat cocoa particles. Deodorized preferred.
*   **Egg White:** Alkaline (pH up to 9 when aged); acts as tenderizer and moisture barrier in velveting.
*   **Baking Soda (Sodium Bicarbonate):** Alkaline tenderizer; creates porosity via CO2 release when heated; accelerates browning.
*   **Salt:** Increases water-holding capacity of meat proteins.
*   **Lime Juice:** Contains oils that cause clouding (Uzo effect) and acts as a nucleation site for freezing. Add last.
*   **Vanilla:** Flavoring. Use whole beans or sugar; avoid extracts (water content).
*   **Lecithin:** Emulsifier (<0.5%).

# Strong Opinions & Recommendations

*   **Wine Name:** "Booza" is the best name for a wine; genius.
*   **Meringues:** Good snacks for sweet tooth due to high air content and low caloric density per volume.
*   **Kale:** Do not eat raw; prefer sautéed with onion, garlic, acid, and salt.
*   **Chocolate Making:** "Crap in, crap out." Quality of nibs is critical.
*   **Baking Soda on Meat:** While it browns meat fast (like pretzels), the taste is "god awful terrible" in large amounts.
*   **Carbonation:** Air is the enemy. Minimize headspace. Higher alcohol = higher pressure needed.
*   **Pre-made Cocktails:** Shaking a pre-diluted, frozen cocktail in a core container with strainer springs is a viable alternative to fresh shaking.
*   **Lemoncello:** The cloudiness issue is likely due to oil precipitation (Uzo effect), but the exact relationship between warm vs. cold syrup addition and bitterness is unproven.
