[CVAP (Convective Vacuum Assisted Performance) Ovens] — Use as a specialized bain-marie with independent controls for water temperature, oven temperature, and humidity.

[CVAP Fish Technique] — Cook fish without a bag and set the temperature 10–15°C higher than the target internal temperature.

["Sucking wind"] — Increase cook times when frequently opening CVAP doors to compensate for heat loss via air exchange.

[CVAP Tortilla Chip Storage] — Use low humidity settings to prevent chips from drying out.

[Immersion Circulator Maintenance] — Use CLR (Calcium Lime Rust) tablets to remove calcium deposits and file spade terminals to prevent electrical resistance and heat-induced failure.

[Low-Temperature Chicken ("Persistent Pinking")] — Avoid high vacuum levels to prevent marrow from being pulled from hollow bones and permanently staining the meat red.

[Sous Vide Beets] — Peel the vegetable before bagging to prevent earthy skin flavors from leaching into the flesh.

[Milk Skins (Yuba)] — Heat milk to 80–90°C on a placid surface to allow proteins to aggregate on fat droplets and polymerize into a film.

[Milk Skin Layering] — Remove the first delicate layers immediately to prevent them from altering the chemical composition of the remaining milk.

[Pressure Cooked Asabuco] — Cook at 15 psi for 25 minutes using a thin sauce and a natural pressure release to prevent the meat from drying out or blowing apart.

[Knife Edge Geometry] — Use Japanese stones to produce a polished "mirror edge" and American grits to create micro-serrations for aggressive cutting through items like tomatoes.

[Sharpening Grit Scales] — Do not compare American and Japanese grit sizes 1:1 due to differing measurement standards.
