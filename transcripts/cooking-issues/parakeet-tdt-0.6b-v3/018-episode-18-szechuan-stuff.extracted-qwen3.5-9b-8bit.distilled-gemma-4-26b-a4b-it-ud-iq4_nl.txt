[Double-Sear Sous Vide Protocol] — Sear meat both before and after immersion (chilling the meat after the pre-sear) to initiate Maillard reactions inside the bag and ensure a final crust.

[Nitrous Oxide (ISI) Infusion] — Rapidly extract flavor from porous ingredients by pressurizing them in liquid with N2O chargers and venting the pressure quickly to force gas out.

[Cocoa Extraction] — Use highly porous Valrhona cocoa nibs for infusions, or melt bar chocolate with liquor and freeze it to separate the fat to extract bitterness via a fat-washing-style mechanism.

[Sancho Peppers] — Achieve a moderate tingling sensation by using these green peppercorns, specifically selecting varieties with "double balls" (two internal green spheres).

[Sichuan Peppercorn Distillation] — Avoid attempting to distill the numbing/tingling sensation, as the compounds responsible are not volatile.

[PolyScience Immersion Circulators] — For the Model 71, use an external thermocouple for calibration and set the thermostat to 200°C to prevent premature shut-off; additionally, sand off "Sous Vide" labels to avoid health inspector scrutiny.

[Xanthan Gum and Oil Thickening] — Use xanthan gum at 0.25%–0.4% for water-based systems, but use mono- and diglycerides (such as Glec by Feron Texturas) or emulsions to thicken oils, as xanthan will not interact with fat.

[Astringency Reduction] — Reduce tannins in fruits or walnut liquors by freezing, drying, or using low-tannin red-skinned walnut varieties to bond the compounds.

[Enzymatic Juice Clarification] — Use Pectinex (Smash XXL or SPL) for direct clarification of orange juice, but use agar for pineapple juice to prevent its proteolytic enzymes from destroying gelatin.
