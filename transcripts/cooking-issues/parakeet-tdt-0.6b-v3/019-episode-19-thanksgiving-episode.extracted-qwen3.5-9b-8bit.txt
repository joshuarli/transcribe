# Candy Making: Pumpkin Maple Caramel
*   **Alcohol Addition:** Adding bourbon to caramel does not negatively affect texture if cooked to the target temperature.
*   **Evaporation:** At high temperatures (approx. 240°F), 99% of alcohol evaporates.
*   **Texture Determinant:** Candy texture is determined by water content, which is guaranteed by reaching the specific target temperature, not the initial amount of water or alcohol added.
*   **Target Temperature:** Standard caramel is cooked to 240°F (approx. 115°C). Pumpkin puree may lower this slightly, but the target remains in the 200–240°F range.
*   **Smoking:**
    *   **PolyScience Smoking Gun:** Can be used to inject smoke into cooling caramel. It is a "beefed up" version of a vacuum cleaner.
    *   **Recommendation:** For a traditional smoked flavor rather than a hint, use a cold smoker.
    *   **Cold Smoker Setup:** Remove the smoke source from the chamber and run smoke through a pipe packed with ice or cooled by running water over it.

# Sous Vide Sweet Potatoes
*   **Enzyme:** Beta-amylase converts starch to sugar.
*   **Process:**
    1.  Cut sweet potatoes into pieces.
    2.  Vacuum seal in a bag (with optional oil).
    3.  **Safety Note:** This pre-treatment is not safe for consumption until the temperature is ramped up to kill potential bacteria.
    4.  **Step 1 (Activation):** Cook at 40–45°C (104–113°F) for 4.5 hours to activate beta-amylase and break down starch.
    5.  **Step 2 (Enzyme Release):** Pectin methylesterase (cell wall strengthening enzyme) requires 60–65°C (140–149°F) to liberate and touch cellular tissues.
    6.  **Step 3 (Cooking):** Slowly ramp temperature from 45°C to simmering to cook the potato through.
*   **Time:** Cooking in a bag takes longer than roasting or boiling due to the absence of excess water; expect times similar to roasting.
*   **Result:** Potatoes retain shape (no shriveling), have no flavor loss/addition, and increased sweetness.
*   **Equipment:** Immersion circulator.

# Transglutaminase ("Meat Glue")
*   **Function:** Enzyme that glues different proteins together.
*   **Application:** Can glue chicken skin to chicken breast, chicken skin to skirt steak (for chicken-fried steak), etc.
*   **Usage:**
    *   Sprinkle powder on pieces of meat.
    *   Press pieces together.
    *   Rest for 4 hours.
*   **Storage:** Store in freezer; lasts >1 year.
*   **Testing:** Smell of raw meat should smell like a "wet wool sweater" during reaction (smell dissipates upon eating).
*   **Brand/Source:** Activa RM by Ajinomoto. Available at Aji Foods USA, Le Sanctuare, Terra Spice.
*   **Cost:** Approx. $80/kg.

# Microwave Physics & Experiments
*   **Visualization:** Neon lamps in foam core can visualize electromagnetic wave patterns and energy intensity distribution.
*   **Load Effect:** Placing water (a load) inside changes the radiation distribution pattern.
*   **Uneven Heating:** Microwaves create standing waves; carbonized areas absorb energy preferentially, leading to hot spots and fire risk.
*   **Speed of Light Calculation:**
    *   **Method:** Place a slab of chocolate in microwave on high to establish a standing wave.
    *   **Measurement:** Measure distance between melted grid points (high power regions).
    *   **Calculation:** Distance = half wavelength. Wavelength = 2 × distance. Speed of light = Frequency × Wavelength.
    *   **Result:** Can calculate speed of light within 3% accuracy using a household ruler, microwave, and chocolate.
*   **Frequency:** Microwaves operate at approx. 2.4 GHz.
*   **Safety Warning:** Do not experiment with microwave doors or mesh integrity.

# Centrifuge Safety & Operation
*   **Risk:** Aluminum buckets fatigue and can blow apart at high speeds (e.g., 4,000g).
*   **Standard Safety:** Never run centrifuges with lids open.
*   **Dave Arnold's Method (For Video):**
    *   Equipment: Labconco Centrovap (clear top, stroboscope).
    *   Modification: Defeated safety mechanisms.
    *   Reinforcement: Secured top with 0.5-inch thick Lexan and 4,000 lb rated ratchet straps.
    *   Observation: Used stroboscope with variable speed to freeze bucket motion.

# Thanksgiving Turkey: Food Safety & Aging
*   **Historical Context:** Hanging birds at ~50°F (10°C) for days was a traditional aging technique to tenderize meat via enzyme action and improve flavor.
*   **Spoilage Factors:**
    *   Cool, dry air (low humidity) encourages a dry pellicle coating, inhibiting bacterial growth.
    *   Garlic has minor antimicrobial properties.
    *   Salt on the skin helps preservation.
    *   Proper butchering leaves muscle relatively sterile.
*   **Warning:** Do not hang birds shot with lead pellets penetrating intestines.
*   **Stuffing Safety:**
    *   **Problem:** Stuffing inside a bird increases cook time, risking undercooking the center.
    *   **Solution:** Pre-cook stuffing separately.
    *   **Technique:** Place stuffing in a Ziploc bag (compressed to <1 inch thickness).
    *   **Parameters:** Circulate at 57°C (135°F) for 1 hour to 1 hour 20 minutes.
    *   **Outcome:** Pasteurizes eggs and other ingredients without damaging texture/flavor.
    *   **Execution:** Stuff the bird with hot stuffing directly from the bag. This gives the bird a "jump start" on cooking.
    *   **Cavity Prep:** Salt the inside of the bird cavity to kill bacteria and enhance flavor.

# Advanced Turkey Cooking Technique (Dave Arnold)
*   **Preparation:** "Glove boning" – remove all bones, leaving the turkey whole (a sack).
*   **Goal:** Reduce cook time to ~2 hours to prevent fibrous texture from low-temp cooking.
*   **Temperature Targets:**
    *   Legs: 65°C (149°F).
    *   Breasts: 64–65°C (147–149°F).
*   **Method:**
    1.  Create an aluminum skeleton inside the boned turkey.
    2.  Pump hot oil through the skeleton to cook the interior/legs first (approx. 1 hour).
    3.  Drop the whole bird into hot fat.
    4.  Cook at slightly lower temperature.
    5.  Cool down.
    6.  **Finishing:** Spray with hot fat and crisp in a fryer.
*   **Equipment:** Two immersion circulators (one for inside/skeleton, one for outside).
*   **Bird Type:** Heritage breed (e.g., Narraganset).
    *   **Characteristics:** Slower growth, better meat quality, lower feed conversion ratio.
    *   **Sourcing:** Small farms, processed nicely, shipped frozen.
    *   **Cost:** Higher due to labor and small-scale production.
