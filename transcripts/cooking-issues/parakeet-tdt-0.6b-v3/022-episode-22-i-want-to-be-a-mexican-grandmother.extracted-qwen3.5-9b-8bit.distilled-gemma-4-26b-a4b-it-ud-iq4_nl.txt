[Soy Lecithin and Methylcellulose for Vegan Baking] — Incorporate 0.5% to 1% powdered soy lecithin via stick blender to provide necessary emulsification, or use methylcellulose and sodium carboxymethylcellulose to mimic egg protein through heat-induced gelation and viscosity that ungels upon cooling.

[Converted/Parboiled Rice] — Utilize grains that have undergone a boil-cool-mill cycle to trigger starch retrogradation/recrystallization, which "presets" the grains to prevent them from rupturing during long cooking periods.

[TDS and Refractometry] — Use refractometry to measure Total Dissolved Solids (TDS) to quantify extraction, while noting that this metric tracks the volume of dissolved material rather than the specific chemical composition of the extract.

[Nixtamalization] — Treat corn with an alkaline agent such as calcium hydroxide or Thai Red Lime Paste to pre-gelatinize starch for cohesive dough formation, soften husks for easier milling, and increase the bioavailability of niacin and calcium.

[Metate and Mano] — Employ these stone grinding tools for nixtamalized corn to avoid the mechanical damage that high-speed blenders suffer when processing the material.
