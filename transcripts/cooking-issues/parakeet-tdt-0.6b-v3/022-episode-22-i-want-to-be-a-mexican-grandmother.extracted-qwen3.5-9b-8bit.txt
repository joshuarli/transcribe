# Egg Substitutes in Baking
*   **Problem:** Commercial liquid egg substitutes (e.g., Egg Beaters) fail in baking because they lack **lecithin** (an emulsifier) and sufficient fat.
*   **Composition of Liquid Substitutes (e.g., Egg Beaters):**
    *   99% egg white.
    *   Additives: Color, spice, salt, onion powder, xanthan gum, guar gum.
    *   Function of Additives: Xanthan and guar modify egg white properties to prevent hardening and provide texture when poured into a pan (suitable for scrambling/omelets).
*   **Solution for Baking:**
    *   Add **soy lecithin** to liquid egg substitutes.
    *   **Ratio:** Approximately 0.5% to 1% by volume (or egg weight).
    *   **Method:** Use powdered soy lecithin; blend with a stick blender to incorporate without excessive heating.
*   **Composition of Powdered Egg Substitutes (Vegan):**
    *   Modified starches (tapioca, potato starch).
    *   Calcium carbonate (leavening agent).
    *   Citric acid.
    *   Sodium carboxymethylcellulose (CMC): Modified cellulose providing viscosity.
    *   Methylcellulose: Provides viscosity and **gelation** when heated (provides structure while batter is hot); ungels when cooled.
*   **Technique:** Methylcellulose acts similarly to egg protein by gelling during heating to set the batter structure before cooling.

# Pig Milk and Cheese
*   **Question:** Why is there no pig milk or pig cheese, given the existence of milk from yaks, camels, cows, goats, and sheep?
*   **Reasoning:**
    *   **Physiology:** Pigs are "meat machines" designed to convert garbage into meat rapidly. Unlike cows, sheep, or goats, they are not multi-use animals (no wool, limited leather/bristles compared to meat yield).
    *   **Lactation Cycle:** Pigs stop lactating immediately after weaning piglets to breed again.
    *   **Yield:** A pig produces significantly less milk per body weight than a cow.
        *   Average pig yield: ~13 lbs/day.
        *   Average cow yield: ~65 lbs/day.
    *   **Economic Viability:** Farmers use the milk to feed the piglets. To produce pig milk for humans, one would need to mechanically feed piglets alternative food and pay farmers to withhold the milk.
*   **Hypothetical Experiment:** Dave suggests paying a farmer the full cost of a litter of pigs to purchase the sow's milk for tasting and cheesemaking.
*   **Human Milk Context:** Dave notes human breast milk is a "super precious commodity" (liquid gold) for working parents and is not a viable restaurant ingredient due to emotional attachment and scarcity.

# Rice Washing, Soaking, and Cooking Methods
*   **Rinsing:**
    *   **Purpose:** Removes excess bran, surface starch, dirt, rocks, twigs, and milling aids (historically talc used for whiteness).
    *   **Visual Cue:** Water turns cloudy quickly due to soluble starch release.
    *   **Enriched Rice:** Rinsing removes added nutrients; some enriched rice is coated with wax/oil to retain nutrients, making rinsing less effective or undesirable.
    *   **Sticky Rice (Japanese):** Must be rinsed until water runs clear to prevent grains from glopping together.
    *   **Indian Cooking:** Often cooked in excess boiling water; rinsing is less critical as starch is diluted, unless the rice is dirty.
    *   **Rinse-Free Rice:** Newer milling technology removes starch/bran coating without rinsing.
*   **Soaking:**
    *   **Benefits:** Reduces fuel usage (water absorbed before cooking), shortens cook time, and ensures even cooking.
    *   **Prevention of Splitting:** Prevents the outside from cooking before the inside is done.
    *   **Sticky Rice (Steamed):** Essential for allowing moisture to penetrate the grain during steaming.
*   **Parboiling (Converted Rice):**
    *   **Process:** Whole rice (in hull) is boiled, cooled (starch retrogrades/recrystallizes), and then milled.
    *   **Effect:** Starches are "preset" or strengthened. Grains remain firm, bounce, and do not rupture/break apart even when cooked in large amounts of water for long periods.
    *   **Nutrition:** Increases nutrient content by boiling in the hull (transferring nutrients from hull to endosperm).
    *   **Note:** Traditional parboiled rice does not necessarily cook faster than non-parboiled; pre-boiled/dried varieties (e.g., Minute Rice) do.

# Aerobia Coffee Machine
*   **Device:** Hand-operated piston coffee maker (approx. $40).
*   **Mechanism:** User drives a piston to force hot water through coffee puck, increasing pressure.
*   **Espresso Definition:**
    *   Requires ~195°F (90°C) and ~9 bars of pressure.
    *   Aerobia operates at ~175°F with moderate hand pressure.
*   **Verdict:** Does **not** make true espresso.
    *   **Chemistry:** Brewing kinetics (extraction rate and composition) differ significantly at lower temperatures and pressures.
    *   **Outcome:** Likely produces a "mocha pot" style coffee (short, strong) but lacks the specific extraction profile of espresso.
*   **Coffee Quality Metrics:**
    *   **Total Dissolved Solids (TDS):** Measured via conductivity; indicates *how much* is extracted.
    *   **Refractometry:** Measures TDS via light refraction; preferred by some for precision.
    *   **Dave's Critique:** Measuring only TDS is insufficient. Different temperatures/pressures extract different compounds. Even at 10% extraction, the *composition* of the 10% varies. True quality requires sensory evaluation (taste, smell, sight) rather than just metrics.

# Nixtamalization
*   **Definition:** Process of treating corn (maize) with an alkaline solution (calcium hydroxide/slaked lime) via boiling and soaking.
*   **Agents:**
    *   **Calcium Hydroxide (Slaked Lime):** Used in Mexico/Central America.
    *   **Thai Red Lime Paste:** Contains calcium hydroxide; used in Thai cooking for frying batters (claimed to increase crispiness, though Dave's tests showed no significant crispness increase, only flavor change).
*   **Functional Benefits:**
    1.  **Milling:** Softens the husk and makes the kernel easier to grind (historically using a *metate* and *mano*).
    2.  **Dough Structure:** Pre-gelatinizes starch and allows the starch to hold more water, creating a cohesive dough (*masa*) without gluten.
    3.  **Nutrition:** Releases **niacin** (preventing pellagra) and increases **calcium** bioavailability.
*   **Flavor:** Imparts a characteristic "lye" flavor (reminiscent of pretzels or tortillas) distinct from un-nixtamalized cornmeal.
*   **Experimental Applications:** Dave has attempted nixtamalization on barley, rye, and wheat.
    *   **Success:** Achieved the characteristic alkaline flavor in rye.
    *   **Failure:** Nastasha disliked the rye flavor.
    *   **Equipment:** Requires a stone grinder (*metate* and *mano*). Standard blenders (Vitamix, Cuisinart) struggle and can be damaged.
*   **Popcorn:** Not suitable for nixtamalization; does not mill well.
*   **Goal:** Dave aims to master hand-forming tortillas and learning Spanish to become a "Mexican grandma."
