[SHORT RIB SAUCE REDUCTION] — Over-reducing sauce to a "gloppy" consistency before bagging prevents dilution from juices released by the meat during sous vide.

[JUICE REABSORPTION] — Allowing a sous vide bag to sit at room temperature for 20 minutes before an ice bath allows the meat to reabsorb released juices.

[SAUCE RE-ABSORPTION] — Re-therming meat with sauce in a Ziploc bag at 55–56°C for 2–4 hours forces the meat to reabsorb the sauce.

[LOW-TEMP TENDERLOIN COOKING] — Cooking beef tenderloin between 30–40°C (86–104°F) for less than 1.5 hours prevents a fibrous texture caused by the lack of connective tissue.

[RIB STEAK SERVICE PREP] — Dropping a sous vide bath to 50°C (122°F) one hour before service lowers the internal temperature of thick rib steaks for easier final searing.

[REVERSE SPHERIFICATION CHEMICALS] — Using Calcium Lactate Gluconate (2.0%–2.5%) instead of Calcium Lactate avoids a bitter/salty taste in the spheres.

[SPHERE ISOTONIC STORAGE] — Storing spheres in their original puree creates an isotonic environment that prevents osmosis from leaching flavor, color, or acidity.

[SLOW COOKER SOUS VIDE] — Utilizing the "Warm" setting (~74°C/165°F) on a slow cooker allows for sous vide or confit cooking using polyethylene Ziploc bags.

[EFFEVERSCENT TABLETS] — Using a manual or hydraulic tablet press to compress an acid (Citric/Malic/Tartaric) and a base (Sodium Bicarbonate) creates fizzy culinary tablets.

[MALTODEXTRIN VARIETIES] — Use EnsoRite Tapioca Maltodextrin to turn oils into powders via long glucose chains, or DE19 (Dextrose Equivalent 19) to provide ~19% sweetness for savory ice creams.

[ISOMALT] — Substitute Isomalt for sugar in marshmallows to provide necessary structure without excessive sweetness.

[GOOSE COMPONENT COOKING] — Cook goose breasts separately at 57–58°C for 45 minutes before searing to achieve a different texture than the legs, which should be cooked confit-style.
