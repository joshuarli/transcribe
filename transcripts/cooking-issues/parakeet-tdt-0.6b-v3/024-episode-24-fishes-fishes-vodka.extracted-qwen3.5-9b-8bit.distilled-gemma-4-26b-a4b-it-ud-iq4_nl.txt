[Nitrous Oxide Infusion (iSi Siphons)] — Use pressure to force liquid into porous solids, but increase product volume and infusion time for water-based liquids like vinegar because nitrous oxide does not enhance solubility.

[Flash-Infused Pickling] — Inject liquid into the air holes of vegetables and use cold temperatures during the pressure release to minimize cellular bubbling and prevent a floppy texture.

[Post-Vent Infusion] — Allow several minutes of resting after venting a siphon to let flavors fully develop in a rapid infusion.

[Amylase Starch Conversion] — Add alpha or beta amylase to settled sweet potato starch to enzymatically convert starches into sugars for caramelization.

[Starch-Syrup Separation] — Boil a starch mixture until evaporation occurs to separate sugary syrup from settled starch, producing a unique translucent caramel.

[High-Yield Steam Distillation] — Avoid vacuum-assisted setups when maximizing essential oil yield, as lower boiling temperatures can reduce the efficiency of cell rupture.

[Low Methoxy Pectin (LMA) Setting] — Add calcium lactate or gluconate to facilitate setting in LMA pectin, enabling jams with significantly lower sugar and acid concentrations.

[AeroPress Reverse Brew] — Use polyester or metal filters while brewing upside down to retain coffee oils and fines for a mouthfeel similar to a French Press.

[Ionic Strength Modification] — Substitute NaCl with KCl or MgCl2 to manipulate protein tertiary structure and volatile solubility, though be mindful that the divalent MgCl2 may cause coagulation.

[Polyphosphate Water Binding] — Use polyphosphates to more aggressively alter water binding in proteins compared to standard salts.

[Taro Oxalate Neutralization] — Cook taro with baking soda to neutralize calcium oxalate and prevent a "sharp needle" sensation in the mouth.

[Tapioca Expansion] — Leverage the "kettle drum" shaped starch of manioc/cassava to achieve superior expansion in puffed snack products.

[Liquor Carbonation] — Use a Twist & Sparkle system rather than a SodaStream when carbonating spirits to prevent excessive foaming.
