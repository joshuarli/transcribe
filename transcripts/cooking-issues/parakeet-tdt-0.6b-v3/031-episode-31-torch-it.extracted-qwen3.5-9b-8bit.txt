# Culinary Notes: Cooking Issues Podcast

## Techniques & Parameters

### Poached Eggs (Immersion Circulator)
*   **Runny Poached Egg:**
    *   **Temp:** 62°C (143.5°F)
    *   **Time:** 1 hour (can be reduced to 45 minutes)
    *   **Holding:** Can drop temp to 60°C/59°C and hold for hours; thin white separates from thick white upon cracking.
*   **Creamy Poached Egg:**
    *   **Temp:** 63°C (145.5°F)
    *   **Time:** 1 hour
    *   **Holding:** Drop to 60°C; yolk is creamy but still runs (good for consommé).
*   **"Hard-Boiled" Style (Soft Yolk):**
    *   **Step 1:** Simmering water for 4–5 minutes.
    *   **Step 2:** Transfer to 70°C (158°F) water.
    *   **Time:** 1 hour.
    *   **Result:** Yolk is perfectly yellow and creamy; not overcooked.
*   **Serving:** Pass through simmering water immediately before service to brighten the white (applies to 95% of eggs).
*   **Note on Oven Method:** Evaporative cooling in an oven drops internal egg temp by 2–3°C, allowing 65°C (149°F) to yield a runny yolk (contrary to Ervatis's claim that 65°C is perfect without this factor).

### Sous Vide Searing Methods
*   **Pre-Sear Strategy:** Sear before water bath to kill bacteria and start crust development; re-sear after water bath to restore crust (which is lost during low-temp cooking).
*   **Temperature Management:** Drop water bath temp significantly before searing to prevent overcooking meat while achieving high surface heat.
    *   *Example:* If cooking steak to 130°F, drop bath to 50°C (122°F) for 30 minutes prior to searing.
*   **Blowtorch Usage:**
    *   **Gas Types:**
        *   *Acetylene:* Too hot; imparts fuel taste (e.g., "god awful" taste on fish). Not recommended.
        *   *Propane/Butane:* Commercial grades often contain odorants (methylmercaptan/ethylmercaptan). Incomplete combustion leads to "torch taste" (sulfur smell), especially on fatty meats.
        *   *MAPP Gas:* Hotter; available at Home Depot; odorant status unknown but potentially cleaner.
        *   *Clean Butane:* Pipe lighters/cigar lighting butane have fewer odorants and smell less.
    *   **Combustion Control:**
        *   Use a grid (e.g., chinois) or Nichrome wire to heat up and ensure complete combustion, eliminating torch taste.
        *   *Warning:* Heating a chinois ruins it; requires buying separate Nichrome wire.
    *   **Roofing Torch:** Can achieve complete combustion with no torch taste but scorches work surfaces and creates uneven crusts. Risk of fire.
    *   **Crust Depth:** Hyper-intense heat may not produce the same Maillard products as normal heat. Requires a specific depth of crust; torching risks incinerating the top before achieving thickness or creating uneven spots.
    *   **Recommendation:** Use torch only for touch-ups (e.g., around bird thighs/wings), not as primary searing method.

### Hydrocolloid Stabilization (Preventing Curdling)
*   **Context:** Preventing dairy curdling in high-acid environments (e.g., Lilikoi/passion fruit).
*   **Carrageenan & Locust Bean Gum (LBG):**
    *   **Iota Carrageenan:** Pudding-like texture; suitable for high acid.
    *   **Kappa Carrageenan:** Rigid texture; **avoid** in high acid (potential carcinogenic concerns at very low pH, though unproven; also undesirable texture).
    *   **Ratio:** Use Iota + LBG for structure without excessive firmness.
*   **Cornstarch:**
    *   Effective at preventing egg yolk curdling when overheated (e.g., Hollandaise).
    *   Can be mixed into dairy before combining with acid.
*   **Pre-gelling:** Pre-gel the dairy before adding acid.

### Chocolate Tempering (Sous Vide)
*   **Method:** Place chocolate in temper in a sous vide bag, vacuum seal, and submerge in water bath set to exact melting point.
*   **Temperature Control:** Maintain accuracy within 0.1°C to keep chocolate in temper.
*   **Execution:** Move bag around to execute temperature ramps if needed.
*   **Caution:** Ensure no water contacts the chocolate upon removal. Use a pastry bag instead of a standard bag for easier handling.

### Rapid Infusion Equipment
*   **Whippers vs. Soda Siphons:**
    *   **Recommendation:** Buy whippers only. Soda siphons are specialized, have thin necks (hard to fill), draw from the bottom (risk of losing carbonation via spray head), and are harder to clean.
    *   **Capacity:**
        *   *1L:* Better for making many drinks; requires more CO2 chargers.
        *   *0.5L:* Standard for recipes/tests; sufficient for home use.
    *   **Usage Note:** If using a 1L whipper with small product volume, add an extra CO2 charger.

### DIY Immersion Circulator Specs
*   **Control Loop:** Must use **PID Control** (Proportional-Integral-Derivative) to prevent overshooting and oscillation.
    *   *DIY Tip:* Use Arduino code (search "Sylvia PID Arduino") or buy a pre-made PID controller (~$35).
*   **Pump:** Aquarium pumps or small recirculation pumps (~$15). Can bubble air or pump water.
*   **Heater:**
    *   Target: ~1000 Watts.
    *   Higher wattage = faster response time.
    *   Ensure electrical outlet can handle amperage (standard 15-amp plug limit).
*   **Precision/Accuracy:**
    *   **Accuracy:** Within 0.3°C of target.
    *   **Precision:** Dead on within 0.1°C (repeatability).
*   **Relay Type:** Use **Solid State Relay** (~$20) instead of mechanical relay to avoid audible clicking and wear.

## Ingredients & Functional Roles

*   **Agar:** A chemical (derived from seaweed); often confused by laypeople.
*   **Carrageenan (Iota vs. Kappa):**
    *   *Iota:* Pudding texture, stable in acid.
    *   *Kappa:* Rigid, unstable in acid, potential safety concerns at low pH.
*   **Locust Bean Gum (LBG):** Stabilizer used with carrageenan in ice creams.
*   **Cornstarch:** Thickener/stabilizer; prevents egg yolk curdling in emulsions (Hollandaise).
*   **Methocel F50:** Whipping agent; creates dense mousses (like shaving cream); acts as an egg substitute.
*   **Preserved Lemons:** Flavor derived from specific strains of **yeast** that survive high salt environments.
*   **Tropical Fruits (South Dade):**
    *   *Chipoticaba:* Grape-like, musky, grows on tree bark.
    *   *Canistel:* Sweet potato taste, egg custard texture; excellent for ice cream.
    *   *Black Sapote:* "Chocolate pudding fruit"; dark, creamy; excellent for ice cream.
    *   *Lilikoi (Passion Fruit):* High acid; requires stabilization (carrageenan/LBG or starch) for custards/creme brûlée.
