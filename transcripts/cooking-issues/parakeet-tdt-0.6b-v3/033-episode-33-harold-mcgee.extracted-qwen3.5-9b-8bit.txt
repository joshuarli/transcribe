# Techniques & Parameters

*   **Rotovap Distillation (Fresh Herbs):**
    *   **Goal:** Distill fresh herbs (e.g., Thai basil, cilantro, cucumber, orange) immediately after distillation to preserve volatile aromas that degrade in water-based distillates.
    *   **Temperature:** Set chiller as low as possible. For alcohol, set around **-20°C**.
    *   **Method:** Do not use automatic distillation; constantly force the distillation to achieve the fastest rate.
    *   **Post-Process:** Immediately drink or use the distillate.
*   **Rotovap Distillation (Peppers):**
    *   **Selection:** Choose fresh, floral red peppers to capture floral notes that do not degrade through the process.
    *   **Horseradish:** Distill to capture the pungent principle, which creates an intensely pungent, long-lasting product.
    *   **Cleaning:** Must completely break down and clean all grease from rotovap joints between batches to prevent contamination, especially when switching from pungent/aromatic ingredients.
*   **Yogurt Making:**
    *   **Pre-heating:** Heat milk to **180°F (82°C)** for **30 minutes**.
    *   **Purpose:** Denatures whey proteins (lactoglobulin), providing body and water-retaining ability. Without this step, the yogurt will curdle and lack thick consistency.
*   **Sausage Smoking:**
    *   **Target Internal Temperature:** Keep sausage below **140°F (60°C)** during smoking to prevent overcooking and juice loss.
    *   **Method:** Water bath at **140°F (60°C)**, then finish by grilling, frying, or pan-searing to create a crust.
*   **Pork Belly Sous Vide:**
    *   **Temperature Range:** **145°F–149°F (63°C–65°C)**. Lower temperatures (e.g., **140°F/60°C**) require longer times (3–5 days) for tenderness.
    *   **Rind:** Leave the rind on; do not trim. Crisp the skin after cooking.
    *   **Preparation:** Compress the pork belly slightly before cooking to create a flat surface for crisping.
    *   **Muscle Identification:** Identify the oval-shaped muscle (Chinese 5 fingers) which becomes mushy/dry with long cooking; remove and cook separately.
*   **Sweet Potato Film (Ovolato):**
    *   **Acid Addition:** Add vinegar to hot sweet potato puree before drying.
    *   **Effect:** Acid causes limited hydrolysis of starch, creating smaller sugar molecules and starch pieces that plasticize the film, preventing brittleness.
    *   **Boiling Vinegar:** Acetic acid volatilizes at a rate similar to water; pH remains relatively constant during boiling.
*   **Bacon Curing:**
    *   **Time Guideline:** Days per inch of meat thickness.
    *   **Vacuum Bagging:** Accelerates cure penetration.
    *   **Brine Strength:** Adding water (e.g., maple syrup) reduces theoretical brine strength, requiring longer cure times.
*   **Beef Brining & Cooking:**
    *   **Sequence:** Brine raw meat first; do not brine after cooking (causes desiccation).
    *   **Sous Vide Temperature for Brisket:** **131°F (55°C)** is low; higher temperatures are preferred for texture.
    *   **Post-Cook Shocking:** Do not shock cooked meat in an ice bath; it prevents reabsorption of expelled juices.
    *   **Enzymes:** Brining unknits muscle fibers; effect on tenderizing enzymes is unknown but brining before cooking is standard.

# Equipment

*   **Rotary Evaporator (Rotovap):**
    *   **Usage:** Best for fresh herbs, peppers, and horseradish.
    *   **Tradeoffs:** Water-based distillates have fugitive aromas that degrade quickly; requires immediate use.
    *   **Settings:** Manual control preferred over automatic for speed. Chiller set to minimum (approx. -20°C for alcohol).
*   **Circulator:** Highly recommended for precise temperature control in sausage and pork belly cooking to avoid overcooking.
*   **Vacuum Bags:** Useful for accelerating marinade/brine penetration and sous vide cooking.
*   **Jacquard Injector:** Allows for rapid flavor penetration into meat.

# Ingredients & Functional Roles

*   **Reduced Fat Milk:** Often supplemented with whey solids (lactoglobulin). Heating denatures these proteins, improving body and water retention compared to full-fat milk where casein curdles.
*   **Marinade Components:**
    *   **Finely Comminuted Ingredients:** Ground or minced spices/aromatics provide more direct contact and flavor release than whole/chopped items.
    *   **Acids:** Penetrate friable meats (e.g., chicken) but can denature surface proteins excessively if overused, creating a mushy layer. Best used in sauces rather than long marinades unless vacuum-sealed.
    *   **Salts:** Break down into ions, easily penetrating meat to alter water-holding capacity.
    *   **Flavor Molecules:** Larger than salt ions; do not penetrate meat easily; best added to the sauce.
*   **Walnuts:**
    *   **Seed Coat Color:** Lighter seed coats indicate lower tannins/astringency. Darker coats indicate higher tannins.
    *   **Red Walnuts:** Contain anthocyanins instead of tannins; less astringent, delicious, but stain hands.
    *   **Freshness:** Critical factor; older nuts lose quality.
*   **Almonds (California):** Generally lower in oil compared to European varieties; likely due to agronomic yield priorities rather than quality intent.
*   **Pink Salt (Instacure #1):** Contains nitrite, not nitrate. Essential for curing bacon.
*   **Maple Syrup:** Can substitute for water/sugar in wet cures but reduces brine strength.
*   **Bitumen/Shilajit:** Medicinal exudate from Himalayas/Dead Sea; historically mistranslated as "mummy" (ground human remains). Used in Ayurvedic medicine.
*   **Rhubarb:** Paired with Shilajit (mummy) as a historical medicinal combination (Francis I of France).

# Strong Opinions & Recommendations

*   **Thai Iced Tea:** Do not guess the recipe; consult David Thompson or Pim's site.
*   **Rotovap Usage:** Use only for things you can do no other way (fresh herbs, specific peppers, horseradish). Clean grease thoroughly between batches.
*   **Yogurt:** Pre-heating milk to 180°F for 30 minutes is non-negotiable for proper texture.
*   **Marinades:** Put acidic flavors in the sauce; put functional ingredients (salt, sugar) in the marinade/brine. Avoid over-marinating with acid unless vacuum-sealed or sous vide, as it makes meat mushy.
*   **Sausages:** Never overcook. Target internal temp <140°F (60°C). Water bath then sear.
*   **Pork Belly:** Compress before cooking. Remove the specific oval muscle that gets mushy. Leave the rind on.
*   **Beef Brining:** Brine raw meat, not cooked meat. Do not shock cooked meat in ice baths.
*   **Walnuts:** Seek light-skinned or red walnuts for lower astringency. Check freshness.
*   **Almonds:** European almonds are superior in oil content and flavor to California almonds.
*   **Salt:** Many salts taste different in solution (0.8%), but trained tasters struggle to distinguish them consistently.
