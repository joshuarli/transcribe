# Culinary Notes: Cooking Issues Transcript

## Gelling Agents & Polysaccharides
*   **Cinnamon Gelling Effect:**
    *   **Observation:** Adding cinnamon to panforte (a dense, boiled sugar/nut/fruit candy) can cause the mixture to turn into a thick slime.
    *   **Cause:** Likely due to polysaccharides in the bark absorbing water or interacting with the boiled sugar component.
    *   **Specific Compound:** *Cinnamin AX* is a polysaccharide found in cinnamon, though its gelling properties are not well-documented.
    *   **Distinction:** Cassia cinnamon contains coumarin (an anticoagulant), which is chemically unrelated to thickening.
    *   **Recommendation:** If seeking a gelling agent, do not rely on cinnamon. Consider gum arabic (sap-based, protein/gum mix) or other barks like philae powder.
*   **Agar vs. Agar Fluid Gel:**
    *   **Agar Fluid Gel:** Forms a gel that holds water but melts near boiling point.
    *   **Hard Agar:** Melts at a lower temperature; unsuitable for applications requiring heat stability.
*   **Panforte Texture:** Naturally very thick due to boiled sugar; adding water-binding agents exacerbates gumming.

## Pastry & Dough Hydration
*   **Vodka in Dough/Batter:**
    *   **Function:** Alcohol hydrates starches without activating gluten (unlike water). It volatilizes during baking, preventing toughness and excessive chewiness.
    *   **Applications:**
        *   **Pie Crust:** Allows higher liquid content for plasticity without toughness; prevents shattering during rolling.
        *   **Fish and Chips Batter (Heston Blumenthal style):** Prevents gluten formation to maintain crispness; used with extra starch binders.
    *   **Limitation:** 100% inhibition of gluten formation creates a dough that is extremely hard to work (similar to gluten-free doughs).
    *   **Gluten-Free Solution:** Pre-cook (boil) a portion of starch to entangle granules and provide structure for workability.
*   **Shortbread:**
    *   **Composition:** Butter, sugar, tiny bit of flour.
    *   **Technique:** Minimal gluten formation; baked to a hard texture.
    *   **Dave's Opinion:** Shortbread is delicious; "hard tack" style shortbread is unappealing.

## Hydrocolloids & Emulsion Stabilization
*   **Guar Gum Issues:**
    *   **Heat Stability:** Guar loses thickening power when heated (reversible upon cooling).
    *   **Flavor:** Inexpensive guar may have an "off" or "beanie" taste.
    *   **Recommendation:** If taste is an issue, source **TIC Gums Guar Flavor Free (NT2000)**.
*   **Xanthan + Guar System:**
    *   **Problem:** Stable at room temp but splits at ~60°C (140°F) in hot sauces (Hollandaise/Beurre Blanc).
    *   **Mechanism:** Guar loses thickness at heat; Xanthan is more heat-stable but insufficient alone for this specific emulsion failure.
*   **Recommended System for Heat-Resistant Emulsions:**
    *   **Components:** Gum Arabic + Xanthan Gum.
    *   **Product:** **Ticoloid 12 S** or **Ticoloid 13 S** (pre-mix from TIC Gums).
    *   **Roles:**
        *   *Gum Arabic:* Acts as the emulsifier (stable over wide heat/dilution ranges).
        *   *Xanthan:* Acts as a weak gelling agent/stabilizer when sitting.
    *   **Benefit:** Creates a base that can be fortified/thinned without losing consistency; prevents egg protein coagulation issues better than traditional methods.
    *   **Alternative:** Add starch to egg yolk to prevent coagulation.
*   **Methylcellulose Warning:** Gels and firms up when heated; not pourable at high temps.

## Candy Making (Gummies & Sour Patch Kids)
*   **Sour Patch Kids Texture:**
    *   **Base Ingredient:** Straight gelatin.
    *   **Texture Issue:** If becoming solid/crumbly before stretchy, moisture levels are incorrect or drying rate is too fast.
    *   **Process:** Requires slow drying over several days to achieve the correct "poured" look and texture.
    *   **Reference:** *Professional Patisserie* by Kirkland (early 1900s) for cornstarch molding techniques.
*   **Gummy Bears:**
    *   **Base Ingredient:** Starch-molded (not just gelatin).
    *   **Texture Control:** Dependent on precise moisture levels and drying rate. Old bears turn hard; fresh ones have the right texture.
*   **Coating Issues (Citric Acid Bleeding):**
    *   **Cause:** Using a 50/50 mix of citric acid and sugar causes the sugar to leach water from the jelly upon contact.
    *   **Solution:** Spray or paint the coating (using alcohol as a binder) rather than mixing powder directly with the candy.
    *   **Acid Alternatives:** Malic acid, Tartaric acid.

## Meat Glue (Transglutaminase)
*   **Activa RM (Thrombin-based) vs. Activa GS (Gelatin-based):**
    *   **Activa GS:** Uses gelatin; creates a stronger bond; can be painted on for even coverage.
*   **Application Techniques:**
    *   **Target:** Glue connective tissue/collagen, *not* rendered fat. Only "native fat" (unrendered) can be glued.
    *   **Coverage:** Ensure connective tissue touches connective tissue. Use a fine dusting (cocoa duster preferred over sieve) to avoid liquid pooling.
    *   **High Heat:** Bonds hold even at high frying temperatures if applied correctly.
*   **Safety:**
    *   **Bacteria:** Gluing introduces bacteria into the meat interior. Must apply sufficient salt/nitrites immediately to kill surface bacteria.
    *   **EU Status:** Thrombin-based glue is banned in the EU (legislative error regarding valid uses); Transglutaminase is allowed.
*   **Usage:** Effective for boneless hams (gluing muscle layers) and layered bellies (e.g., rabbit + bacon).

## Bread Baking (Oven Spring & Cracking)
*   **Bottom Bursting/Splitting:**
    *   **Primary Cause:** Poor forming technique (lack of a good "skin" on the dough) rather than hydration or steam issues.
    *   **Steam Techniques:**
        *   *Ice Method:* Throws ice into a well-vented oven; steam evaporates too quickly.
        *   *Hotel Pan Steamer:* Drill hole in hotel pan, place over bread on stone, pipe steam from garment steamer for 20-30 seconds, then bake. Mimics professional steam better.
*   **Hydration:** Tested ranges from 60% to 85% with no resolution to the specific cracking issue.

## Seaweed Cultivation & Sourcing
*   **Sourcing:**
    *   **Species:** Wakame, Kombu, Sea Lettuce, Arame, Nori, Kelp.
    *   **Locations:** Asian grocery stores (often unlabeled); Pacific Northwest specific guides.
    *   **Wild Harvesting:** Possible but risky (rotting/debris).
*   **Cultivation:**
    *   **Aquarium:** Certain varieties (Green Laver/Sea Lettuce) can be raised in saltwater aquariums (expensive).
    *   **Scale:** Most seaweeds are too large for normal home hydroponics; commercial farming is standard.
*   **Preparation:**
    *   **Dried:** Must be "refreshed" (rehydrated).
    *   **Fresh:** Can be used immediately.
    *   **Kombu:** Can be used to cure/season duck breast before peeling and slicing for salad (crunchy texture).
    *   **Salads:** Avoid over-soaking to maintain crunch.
*   **Resources:**
    *   *Cooking with Sea Vegetables* by Sharon Ann Rhodes (outdated, 70s/80s).
    *   *Seaweeds of the Pacific Northwest* by Brian Gutschmidt (website: pendiva.com/seaweed).

## Burger Preparation (Dave Arnold's Method)
*   **Meat Mix:** Short rib chuck + ground bacon.
*   **Prep:** Loose pack, relatively thick. Quick fry to set surface.
*   **Cooking Method:**
    1.  **Slow Cook:** Submerge in butter (or beurre blanc style with hyper-reduced beef stock) in a bag at **55°C (131°F)** for a couple of hours.
    2.  **Finish:** Hyper-grill on hardwood coals (surface temp approx. sun surface temp) to create crust.
*   **Bun:** Homemade English muffin (Buffalo style) with caraway seed and salt.
*   **Toppings:**
    *   Cheese: American or Gruyère.
    *   Tomato: Flash-infused with vinaigrette OR pickled green tomatoes (due to lack of decent fresh tomatoes).
    *   Onion: Fresh raw (Dave's preference); Nastasha prefers grilled.
*   **Philosophy:** Avoid long cooking times (gamey/livery notes) and vacuum packing (smashes texture).
