# Equipment & Tools

*   **Thermomix (Thermic)**
    *   **Description:** A multi-functional device combining a blender, food processor blade, scale (in the bowl), and heating element.
    *   **Capabilities:** Can heat and stir simultaneously; useful for hydrocolloids (e.g., keeping gels from cooling/gelling prematurely), making hot foams, and creating cheese sauces that do not burn.
    *   **Tradeoffs:** Not a high-performance blender; expensive (approx. $700 USD, equivalent to two high-end blenders).
    *   **Dave Arnold's Opinion:** Would rather have two high-power blenders (Vita-Preps) than one Thermomix. Notes that European chefs love it due to market availability and price differences, whereas in the US, high-power blenders are more accessible and preferred for blending tasks.
    *   **Status:** No longer distributed in the US; Canadian distributors allegedly ship to the US.
*   **AeroPress**
    *   **Description:** A cheap, piston-based coffee maker.
    *   **Accessory:** Koava disc (fine mesh filter).
    *   **User Feedback:** Richard Kokovich reports liking the Koava disc for his AeroPress.
*   **Centrifuge**
    *   **Application:** Used to blend and spin liquids with enzymes to create crystal-clear infusions (e.g., strawberry tequila, gin justino).
    *   **Acquisition Anecdote:** Dave bought a used unit on eBay for ~$300 to give to Martha Stewart. It broke immediately upon arrival (a $900 repair cost), requiring a replacement.
*   **Combi Ovens**
    *   **Features:** Super fancy ovens capable of steam convection and precise temperature/humidity control.
    *   **Application:** Can theoretically prevent cheesecake cracks by managing moisture and heat better than home ovens.
*   **Vita-Prep**
    *   **Description:** High-power blender.
    *   **Recommendation:** Dave recommends owning multiple units over a Thermomix.
*   **Lowboy**
    *   **Description:** A large, deep sink or basin used in professional kitchens.
    *   **Usage:** Scrubbing out the lowboy at the end of a service is cited as a "litmus test" for a cook's commitment and work ethic.

# Techniques & Parameters

*   **Mummy Juice (Strawberry Tequila)**
    *   **Ingredients:** Strawberries, Reposado tequila, lime juice, ice.
    *   **Process:** Blend strawberries and tequila; pass through a centrifuge with enzymes to achieve crystal clarity; squeeze in lime; stir with ice.
    *   **Origin:** Named after Juan Juato, Mexico, where strawberries are grown and mummies are found.
*   **Gin Justino**
    *   **Ingredients:** Bombay Sapphire gin, bananas, ginger (suggested garnish).
    *   **Process:** Blend gin and bananas; spin in a centrifuge with enzymes.
    *   **Definition:** "Justino" means blending with bananas and spinning in a centrifuge with enzymes.
*   **Low-Temperature Burger Patty (Patty Melt Variation)**
    *   **Method:** Cook burger patty low temperature inside a Ziploc bag with butter.
    *   **Assembly:** Cook like a grilled cheese sandwich with caramelized onions and rye bread; separate components; place un-smashed burger inside.
    *   **Result:** Bread toasts, burger remains intact without being smashed.
*   **Meat Glue Bacon Sheets**
    *   **Process:** Laminade bacon with meat glue; vacuum seal; cut into rings.
*   **Pickle Sheets (Japanese Style)**
    *   **Tool:** Usuba knife.
    *   **Process:** Cut pickles into rings.
*   **Burger "Soup Dumpling" Construction**
    *   **Gelatin Reduction:** Reduce veal stock to a thick, beefy consistency with strong gelatin content.
    *   **Flavoring:** Add ketchup to the reduction.
    *   **Calcium Lactate:** Added to the mixture.
    *   **Sheet Formation:** Set into a sheet.
    *   **Encapsulation:** Cut sheets into rings; throw into alginate to form a protective layer around the gelatin/ketchup sheet.
    *   **Cooking:** Build burger around the encapsulated sheet; low-temp fry in butter; roast over charcoal for a charred exterior.
    *   **Outcome:** Cutting the burger causes the inner gelatin/ketchup layer to explode outward (like a soup dumpling).
*   **Cheesecake Cracking Prevention**
    *   **Dave Arnold's Stance:** No specific universal tip; depends on temperature, water bath usage, and oven variables.
    *   **Brooks Headley's Method:** Bakes in a standard still oven (same as home); covers cracks with cherries.
    *   **Potential Causes:** Top setting/drying out too soon; rapid cooking causing structural split.

# Ingredients & Functional Roles

*   **Enzymes:** Used in centrifuge processes to aid in clarification (strawberry tequila, gin justino).
*   **Meat Glue:** Used to bind bacon into sheets for lamination.
*   **Alginate:** Used to create a tough, heat-proof layer around the gelatin/ketchup sheet in the burger.
*   **Calcium Lactate:** Added to the veal stock/ketchup reduction to aid in setting the gelatin sheet.
*   **Red Vanilla:** Uncured vanilla; does not taste like vanilla until fermented.
*   **Ackee Fruit:** National fruit of Jamaica; poisonous if eaten unripe; pods must open fully for ripeness.
*   **Aki (Ackee):** Related to the above; requires specific ripening to be edible.

**Caller:** Nick Fila (Culinary School Student)
**Question:** Meaning of "Certified Master Chef" title and tips for getting into New York restaurants.
**Answer:**
*   **Certified Master Chef:** A prestigious title requiring a "preposterous battery of tests" (chronicled in *The Soul of a Chef*). There are only ~60-70 in the US. It is not typically sought for line cook jobs but rather for institutional, teaching, or corporate roles.
*   **Getting Hired:**
    *   **References:** Crucial to have someone vouch for you.
    *   **Persistence:** Writing multiple emails and pestering restaurants can work if done relentlessly.
    *   **Staging:** Working for free ("staging") is common. Be willing to do any task ("work like a dog").
    *   **Litmus Test:** Watch how a candidate handles repetitive tasks (e.g., rolling chocolate truffles). Speed and consistency indicate potential.
    *   **School Connections:** Culinary schools often have alumni networks who can vouch for students.

# Personal Anecdotes & Demonstrations

*   **Lucky Rice Festival Plans:** Dave and Nastasha planned to make "Mummy Juice" (strawberry tequila centrifuge) and "Gin Justino" (gin/banana centrifuge) to sell at the festival. They had 12 bottles of each and intended to pour quickly to join the party.
*   **Artichoke Dessert Evolution:** Nastasha attempted an artichoke dessert 10 years ago which was "terrible" (chopped, caramelized, hammered, served on ice cream). She recently recreated it for a museum event (Hebrew food in Italy) and found it "pretty good," noting her evolution as a chef.
*   **French Fry Burrito:** Ben Schwanier tried a French fry burrito at a local Mexican spot and liked it.
*   **Semi-Changa:** A concept from a pilot show for "Stoners." A burrito fried only on the bottom third/half to create a crispy base while keeping the top soft like a burrito. This prevents the top from shattering when eaten like a chimichanga or the bottom from slumping like a burrito.
*   **Martha Stewart Centrifuge:** Dave bought a used centrifuge on eBay for ~$300 to give to Martha Stewart. It broke immediately after he fixed one issue, revealing a $900 problem. Dave joked about not having a "hair shirt" to wear while apologizing to her.
*   **Patty Melt Experiment:** Dave created a "fantabulous fancy" patty melt for Eater.
    *   *Version 1:* Low-temp cooked patty in a Ziploc bag with butter, grilled like a sandwich. Eater rejected it as not "wacky" enough.
    *   *Version 2:* Used meat-glue bacon sheets, ring-cut Gruyere, ring-cut pickles (Usuba knife), and a ring of veal stock/ketchup gelatin encapsulated in alginate. The burger was roasted over charcoal. Cutting it caused the inner gelatin layer to explode outwards.
*   **Hot Hands:** Nastasha has "hot hands," meaning she smears chocolate truffles while rolling them. Dave notes this is a known issue for pastry chefs.
*   **Cleaning the Lowboy:** Brooks emphasizes that scrubbing out the lowboy at the end of the night is a critical quality control step and a test of a cook's attitude. He notes many people burn out when they realize fine dining involves scrubbing sinks at 2 AM.
