# Raw Vegan Diet Experience
*   **Dave's Stance:** Does not believe it is healthier than a moderate diet; considers it a hardship, expensive, and leads to energy loss, grumpiness, and muscle mass loss.
*   **Enzyme Argument:** Disagrees with the premise that consuming raw enzymes is beneficial; humans produce their own enzymes.
*   **Chocolate:** Acknowledges "raw" chocolate (unroasted) tastes like chocolate but has incorrect texture (too soft) because cacao fermentation often exceeds 118°F (sometimes 19°F+), destroying enzymes and preventing proper flavor development without roasting.
*   **Practicality:** Not suitable for picky eaters or those with limited time; requires significant planning and money (e.g., dips/crackers cost ~$8).
*   **Physiological Effects:** Causes rapid sugar highs and crashes if relying solely on raw fruit; leads to feeling weak and passing out tired.
*   **Recommendation:** Not recommended for general adoption.

# Oil Preservation (Deep Frying)
*   **Antioxidant:** Carnosic acid (derived from rosemary) is an effective antioxidant that prevents oil rancidity and breakdown into free fatty acids during storage.
*   **Trade-off:** Commercial rosemary extract is often deodorized (loses rosemary scent) and added in small amounts (parts per million).
*   **Recommendation:** Use rosemary oil (or sage oil) with a few drops added to frying oil.
*   **Experimental Note:** Effectiveness at high frying temperatures regarding lipid oxidation is unproven; would require a controlled experiment comparing oil with/without additive using temperature ramping.

# Meat Freezing & Refreezing
*   **Safety:** Refreezing is **not** a safety issue if the meat was safe initially and not temperature-abused during thawing. Freezing arrests but does not kill bacteria.
*   **Quality:** Refreezing causes significant quality loss due to dehydration and ice crystal formation puncturing cells.
*   **Drip Loss:** Thawed meat releases liquid (drip loss); repeated freeze-thaw cycles worsen tissue breakdown and liquid loss.
*   **Specific Scenarios:**
    *   **Raw Products (Sausage, Dumpling Fillings):** Refreezing is discouraged due to quality loss.
    *   **Cooked Products (Stews, Braises):** Refreezing is acceptable with less quality loss if done correctly.
*   **Best Practices for Refreezing Cooked Meat:**
    *   Use vacuum bags or tightly sealed Ziploc bags to exclude oxygen (prevents rancidity).
    *   Ensure no moisture sublimates out or recrystallizes on the outside (prevents freezer burn).
    *   **Fast Freeze Technique:** Bag meat and submerge in a heavy brine and ice solution to freeze rapidly.

# Salumi (Cured Meats) Differentiation
*   **Fat-to-Lean Ratio:** Primary determinant of texture and mouthfeel.
*   **Particle Size:**
    *   *Emulsified:* Fats and meat fully mixed (e.g., Mortadella).
    *   *Coarse:* Large chunks (e.g., Sopressata, Salame).
    *   *Intermediate:* Hunter sausage, Cacharini.
*   **Seasoning:** Spice and/or wine mixtures define characteristic flavors.
*   **Kneading:** Traditional salami is kneaded with salt to bind finely chopped meat into a compact mass; coarser products require less kneading.
*   **Casing Size:** Thinner casings result in shorter drying times but potentially less complex flavors.
*   **Fermentation & Curing:**
    *   Wild fermentation occurs naturally in many traditional products.
    *   Nitrites/Nitrates added to prevent botulism (bacteria grow, drop pH, create acidic/tart flavor).
    *   Temperature and microflora of the aging environment heavily influence the final product type.

# Popcorn Techniques
*   **Preferred Method:** **Whirly Pop** (lidded hinge with an internal wheel/agitator).
    *   **Mechanism:** Agitates kernels while heating; steam vents allow moisture to escape.
    *   **Heat:** Medium (not so high as to scorch).
    *   **Benefits:** Consistent popping, no chewy kernels, prevents steam buildup.
    *   **Versatility:** Can also be used for roasting coffee beans.
*   **Alternative Methods:**
    *   *Stovetop (Oil):* High heat until few pop, then lower heat. Use splatter screen (not a tight cover) to prevent chewiness caused by trapped steam.
    *   *Microwave:* Variable results depending on kernel variety.
*   **Kernel Varieties:**
    *   *Butterfly:* Standard eating popcorn.
    *   *Mushroom:* Larger surface area, holds coating well (caramel).
    *   *Widows:* Unpopped kernels left at the bottom.
*   **Recommendation:** Buy a Whirly Pop for best results.

# Pressure Cooker Usage (Electric Models)
*   **Pressure Level:** Most electric models run at 15 psi (standard), but verify specific model specs.
*   **Advantages:** No gas throttling required; minimal scorching on the bottom; doesn't heat up the kitchen; saves burner space.
*   **Techniques & Recipes:**
    *   **Stocks:** Excellent for "reinforced" or triple stocks (bones) in ~1 hour.
    *   **Sealed Stock:** If the cooker is non-venting, seal stock in mason jars (similar to Modernist Cuisine technique).
    *   **Egg Bread:** No extra liquid needed; uses egg yolks, baking powder, salt, sugar/soy. Steamed or pressure cooked.
    *   **Garlic:** Pressure cook in milk (~20 mins), blend with oil for pizza sauce.
    *   **Ramps:** Pressure cook wild ramps for ice cream.
    *   **Onions/Horseradish:** Pressure cook then puree for ice cream.
    *   **Mustard Seeds:** Blanch in water, pressure cook in vinegar, add sugar afterwards (prevents scorching from high sugar).
    *   **Hamine Eggs:** Hard boil eggs (6 mins), then pressure cook in hot water for ~1 hour. Whites turn nutty brown; yolks smell like chicken liver.
    *   **Braises (e.g., Pork Shoulder):** Faster than traditional braising.
*   **Liquid Management:** Do not add excessive extra liquid. Reduce wine/alcohol first, then reduce the resulting stock.
*   **Texture Note:** Fibers break apart more (overcooked technically), resulting in a richer, meatier taste but different texture.

# Alkaline Treatments (Nixtamalization & Pretzels)
*   **Nixtamalization:** Cooking corn in calcium hydroxide (lime). Breaks down hull, pre-hydrates, changes flavor/texture for masa.
*   **Pretzel Making:** Requires a basic solution to convert sodium bicarbonate to sodium carbonate (enhances Maillard reaction, creates dark brown crust and specific flavor).
*   **Safety:**
    *   *Calcium Hydroxide:* Safe and effective.
    *   *Sodium Hydroxide (Lye):* Traditional but dangerous for home use with children.
    *   *Potassium Carbonate/Sodium Bicarbonate Solution:* Safe alternative (Consui); used for yellow alkaline noodles.
    *   *Borax:* **Not food safe** in the US (legal in other countries for caviar/fish roe as preservative/texture modifier).
*   **Vegetable Firming (Aderezos):** Calcium hydroxide cross-links pectin (activated by heat via pectin methyl esterase enzyme). Creates firm exterior, mushy interior. Same principle as firming bananas with calcium injection.
*   **Pretzel Specifics:**
    *   **Shape:** Must be twisted (traditional shape affects breakage and baking texture).
    *   **Oil:** Do **not** add oil to pretzel dough (reduces texture to that of a cracker).
    *   **Verification:** Weigh baking soda before and after conversion to sodium carbonate to verify water/CO2 loss.
    *   **Recommendation:** Use lye if available; sodium carbonate is a fine runner-up.
