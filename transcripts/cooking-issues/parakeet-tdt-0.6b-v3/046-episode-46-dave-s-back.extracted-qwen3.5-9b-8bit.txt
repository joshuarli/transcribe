# Cooking Issues: Structured Culinary Notes

## Techniques & Parameters

### Peacock Preparation
*   **Challenge:** Historically considered dry and stringy due to cooking methods of the Roman/Middle Ages.
*   **Solution:** Can be cooked properly with modern techniques to avoid toughness.
*   **Context:** Dave Arnold tasted peacock during a pilot shoot for a cooking-based show (AB).

### Mango Selection & Ripening
*   **Commercial Issue:** Mangoes shipped to New York undergo heat treatment (immersion in hot water) to kill fruit flies/insects.
*   **Consequence:** Heat treatment kills some aromas and prevents natural ripening, resulting in inferior flavor compared to tree-ripened fruit.
*   **Tasting Methodology:**
    *   To exhaustively taste a fruit variety, sample at least **100** types (Dave's standard for citrus, pears).
    *   Sampling **40–50** varieties provides a good idea of the flavor range.
    *   Dave sampled **60–80** types of mangoes in Florida.
*   **Flavor Profiles Observed:**
    *   Creamsicle-like flavors.
    *   Coconut-like flavors.
    *   Distinct categories: Indian-derived, Southeast Asian (Thai-style), Florida hybrids, and Egyptian varieties.

### Papaya Self-Gelling
*   **Phenomenon:** Stored papaya juice forms a jelly/gel at the bottom of containers.
*   **Cause:** Activity of **pectinesterase**, an enzyme in papaya.
*   **Mechanism:** Pectinesterase strengthens pectin molecules (does not necessarily polymerize them), causing the juice to self-gel.
*   **Application:** Could potentially be used to make pie juices that gel on their own, though texture is unknown.
*   **Contrast:** Dave uses the *opposite* enzyme (pectinase) to break down pectin for clarification.

### Iced Coffee Steeping (Cold Brew)
*   **Method:** Add room temperature ground coffee, stir, steep overnight at room temperature, filter through super fine mesh.
*   **Result:** Super rich, deep coffee with top notes; supposedly less bitterness.
*   **Container Shape:**
    *   **Recommendation:** Use **round Cambros** (circular containers).
    *   **Reasoning:** When decanting settled coffee, lifting a round container allows spinning without stirring up settled grounds. Square containers cause corners to mix fluid like a mixer when turned, kicking up particles.
    *   **Impact:** Round containers improve filtration efficiency by allowing clear liquid to be poured off before particles enter the strainer.
    *   **Note:** If stirring occurs before pouring, container shape is irrelevant.

### Vegetable Sous Vide (Vacuum Sealing)
*   **Problem:** Vegetables (e.g., carrots) float to the top of the water bath due to internal air expansion during heating, leading to uneven cooking.
*   **Solution 1 (Hardcore Vacuum):**
    *   Apply a vacuum for **30 seconds longer** than the standard full vacuum setting.
    *   **Benefit:** Removes more air, helping vegetables sink; injects flavorings (oil, juice) deeper into the vegetable.
*   **Solution 2 (Weighting):**
    *   Add stainless steel knives, butter knives, or spoons to the bag.
    *   **Technique:** Seal knives in a bottom section of the bag, then seal vegetables on top to prevent direct contact if desired.
    *   **Effect:** Weights the bag down like a stone, ensuring full submersion.
*   **Cooking Parameters:** Vegetables require high temperatures (**85°C / 185°F** and above) to break down effectively.
*   **Time Consideration:** Cooking in vacuum bags without excess water takes longer than with excess water.

### Dehydrated Fruit Sheets (Fortune Cookie Style)
*   **Goal:** Crispy, flexible sheets that harden when cooled, retaining original color (avoiding dark brown).
*   **Problem:** High-temperature dehydration caramelizes sugar and milk solids, turning the product dark brown. Low-temperature fruit leather remains flexible but doesn't harden.
*   **Solution:** Switch from standard sugar to **isomalt**.
    *   **Property:** Isomalt browns significantly less than sugar at high temperatures.
    *   **Trade-off:** Isomalt is less sweet than sugar.
*   **Alternative:** Use non-caramelizing maltodextrin-based bases (may reduce flexibility).

### Marshmallow Non-Stick Coating
*   **Problem:** Homemade marshmallows stick together after cutting.
*   **Standard Coating:** 1:1 ratio of cornstarch and powdered sugar.
*   **Issue with Standard:** Powdered sugar absorbs atmospheric moisture and marshmallow moisture, becoming tacky over time.
*   **Recommended Technique (Industrial Method):**
    1.  Bake pure cornstarch in the oven to ensure it is completely dry.
    2.  Seal dry cornstarch in quart containers to cool.
    3.  Dust marshmallows with **pure powdered sugar** first (creates a protective layer).
    4.  Toss in the **dry cornstarch**.
    5.  Let sit for **4–5 hours to 1 day**.
    6.  **Mechanism:** Cornstarch acts as a desiccant to dehydrate the outer layer of the marshmallow without absorbing too much into the interior, preventing stickiness while maintaining taste.

### Malt Production (Beer Context)
*   **Process:** Barley is germinated to activate enzymes (**alpha and beta amylases**), then the sprout is killed while retaining enzymes.
*   **Function:** Enzymes convert starch into sugar, which yeast converts to alcohol.
*   **Types:**
    *   **Diastatic Malt:** Contains active enzymes; used in baking to convert starch to sugar for yeast/bacteria food.
    *   **Dry Malt Extract:** Starch fully converted to sugar; dried into powder. Retains malty flavor.
*   **Culinary Use:** Dry malt extract is a fantastic sweetener/ingredient for non-beer applications (e.g., mashed potatoes).

### Oxygen Scavengers
*   **Function:** Packets with higher affinity for oxygen than food, preventing oxidation.
*   **Safety:** Generally food-safe, but specific products vary.
*   **Anecdote:** Oxygen scavengers were used in Reese's Peanut Butter Cups to prevent rancidity. Consumers disliked the taste because the characteristic flavor of the cup relies on a specific level of rancidity. Production was halted due to consumer preference for the "selective oxidation" flavor profile.

### Vacuum Sealing Powders
*   **Issue:** Fine powders (e.g., spray-dried vinegar powder, transglutaminase) can cake, pellet, or disperse during vacuum cycles and gas flushing.
*   **Vacuum Cycle:** Generally safe; particles do not fly out of the bag as the vacuum sucks through a hole dispersed over the chamber.
*   **Gas Flush Issue:** High-pressure gas jets can spray contents across the machine.
*   **Mitigation:**
    *   Use diffuser plates over gas jets.
    *   Turn gas flow and pressure way down.
    *   **Note:** Gas flush may not be necessary if the goal is only to prevent compaction; a tiny bit of gas might suffice.
    *   **Result:** Without gas flush, compaction/clumping is less likely.

## Equipment Discussed

### Cambros (Food Storage Containers)
*   **Types:** Square vs. Round.
*   **Sizes Mentioned:** 4L, 7L, 2L (2 quart).
*   **Recommendation:** Round containers preferred for decanting settled liquids (coffee, juices) to avoid stirring up sediment.

### Vacuum Sealers
*   **Model Mentioned:** MiniPack 31 X Chamber (with gas flush).
*   **Features:** Small unit with larger chamber size relative to footprint; capable of gas flushing (Nitrogen, CO2).
*   **Gas Flush Application:**
    *   **Fish:** Can use CO2 or Carbon Monoxide (Dave advises against CO for home use).
    *   **Chips:** Prevents crushing by maintaining internal gas pressure; prevents oxidation due to dry gas.
*   **Powder Handling:** Requires diffusers or low-pressure settings to prevent spraying during gas flush.

### Immersion Circulator
*   **Usage:** Cooking vegetables sous vide.
*   **Challenge:** Managing floating vegetables (addressed via weighting or hardcore vacuum).

### Enzymes
*   **Papain:** Protein-breaking enzyme in papaya (meat tenderizer). Can cause gelling in specific protein concentrations.
*   **Pectinesterase:** Enzyme in papaya that strengthens pectin, causing self-gelling of juice.
*   **Pectinase:** Enzyme used by Dave to break down pectin for juice clarification (opposite of pectinesterase).
*   **Alpha/Beta Amylases:** Enzymes activated in malt to convert starch to sugar.

## Ingredients & Functional Roles

### Mangoes
*   **Properties:** Wide flavor range (creamsicle, coconut).
*   **Ripening:** Natural ripening is inhibited by commercial heat treatment.

### Papaya
*   **Properties:** High bioactive molecules; contains papain (protease) and pectinesterase.
*   **Function:** Meat tenderizer; causes self-gelling of juices.

### Isomalt
*   **Properties:** Sugar substitute that browns less than sucrose at high temperatures.
*   **Function:** Maintains color in dehydrated fruit sheets; less sweet.

### Dry Malt Extract
*   **Properties:** Powdered form of malted barley with converted starch and malty flavor.
*   **Function:** Sweetener; yeast food; flavoring agent (e.g., in mashed potatoes).

### Gelatin (Recombinant)
*   **Source:** Genes injected into yeast to produce human-derived gelatin (no animal harm).
*   **Properties:** Consistent quality (unlike natural gelatin which varies by source/bloom strength); no animal allergens.
*   **Applications:** Marshmallows, Jell-O.
*   **Vegan Status:** Unclear/Debatable (Dave notes vegans might not use it despite no animal harm).

### Peanut Butter (Rancidity)
*   **Context:** Characteristic taste of Reese's cups is linked to selective oxidation/aging (rancidity).
*   **Implication:** Preventing all oxidation via scavengers can alter desired flavor profiles.

## Strong Opinions & Recommendations

*   **Mangoes:** Commercial heat-treated mangoes are inferior; tree-ripened fruit is vastly superior.
*   **Cambros:** Strongly recommend round containers for cold brew coffee to ensure clean decanting.
*   **Vegetable Cooking:** Weighting vegetables (with knives) or using "hardcore vacuum" is necessary for even sous vide cooking.
*   **Dehydrated Fruit:** Use isomalt instead of sugar to prevent browning in crispy fruit sheets.
*   **Marshmallows:** Do not use a 1:1 cornstarch/sugar mix for long-term storage; use the "bake cornstarch, dust with sugar, then cornstarch" method to dehydrate the exterior.
*   **Gelatin:** Recombinant gelatin offers consistency and ethical benefits (no animal harm) and could revolutionize products like marshmallows and Jell-O if price-competitive.
*   **Papaya:** The self-gelling property of papaya juice is a unique enzymatic trait worth studying.
*   **Rancidity:** Not all oxidation is bad; some flavor profiles (like Reese's cups) rely on it.
