# Techniques & Parameters

### Ikejime (Fish Killing)
*   **Objective:** Prevent rigor mortis damage by depleting ATP in muscles before contraction sets in.
*   **Procedure:**
    1.  **Immediate Brain Spike:** Upon landing/gaffing, immediately spike the brain to stop flopping and reduce stress. (Preferred over whacking over the head or gill cutting first).
    2.  **Spinal Cord Ablation (Shinkanuki):** Cut through the backbone to destroy the spinal column. This stops nerve signals to muscles, reducing ATP usage and preventing hard, damaging rigor.
    3.  **Tail Cut:** Cut the tail to complete the spinal destruction.
    4.  **Bleeding:** Do not cut the fish into chunks before bleeding. Keep the heart pumping to clean out blood. Ideally, bleed in the water at the pier. If using a cooler, ensure it is large enough for the whole fish to allow blood circulation.
    5.  **Gutting:** Gut immediately after ikajime.
    6.  **Cleaning Spinal Vessels:** Use a stiff brush to scrub the underside of the spine membrane where blood vessels run to remove "goopy stuff" that spoils quickly.
    7.  **Cooling:** Pull gills out immediately and ice the fish.
*   **Application:** Recommended for line-caught fish (e.g., bluefish, king mackerel, redfish).
*   **Testing Protocol:** Catch two identical fish; perform ikajime on one, do not on the other. Compare texture and flavor.
*   **Aging:** Test aging duration by tasting immediately after filleting, then again after 1 day. Most oily fish (bluefish, mackerel) should be eaten within 1 day.
*   **Preparation Note:** Do not fillet immediately if the cooler is too small for the whole fish; keep whole to maintain blood flow during bleeding.

### Low-Temperature Reductions (DIY Aspirator)
*   **Goal:** Achieve vacuum reduction without a Rotovap (for delicate aromas/flavors).
*   **Equipment:**
    *   Erlenmeyer flask with sidearm and plug.
    *   Stirring hot plate (used, ~$100).
    *   Aspirator pump (recirculating preferred).
    *   Aspirating nozzles (screw onto faucet, use venturi effect).
    *   Ice bath (critical for maximizing vacuum via water vapor pressure).
*   **Method:**
    *   Use ice water in the aspirator to lower boiling temperature.
    *   Recirculate water to avoid waste (unlike disposable aspirators).
    *   Stirring is essential to prevent bumping and create a vortex for surface area.
*   **Alternative:** DIY setup using a flowjet pump, PVC tubes, and Home Depot pump (~$80-$90), though not explicitly recommended due to safety concerns.

### Milk Pasteurization (Hot Holding)
*   **Context:** Sub-Saharan Africa (Kenya/Uganda) where cold chains are unreliable.
*   **Method:** Heat milk to pasteurization temperatures and hold hot for several days.
*   **Parameters:**
    *   **Temperature:** Pasteurization temps (exact degree omitted in transcript, implied standard pasteurization).
    *   **Duration:** Up to 3 days for acceptable taste; microbiologically indefinite.
    *   **Flavor Profile:** Consumers prefer boiled/hot milk flavor; hot holding preserves this better than cooling then reheating.
    *   **Energy:** Less energy intensive than cooling then reheating.
*   **Equipment:** Low-tech heat exchangers (<$200) that resist fouling.

### Hard Boiled Eggs
*   **Method A (Cold Start):** Place eggs in cold water, bring to boil.
    *   *Pros:* Prevents thermal shock/splitting.
    *   *Cons:* Harder to time accurately; potentially harder to peel.
*   **Method B (Hot Start):** Drop eggs into boiling water.
    *   *Pros:* Accurate timing; easier to peel.
    *   *Cons:* Thermal shock risk.
*   **Recommendation:** If timing is not rigorous, bring water to just below boil, cover, and let sit.
*   **Peeling Tip:** Use a blowtorch to briefly pulse heat on the dry shell after cooking to facilitate peeling.

### Cartouches
*   **Definition:** Circular parchment paper with a smaller circle cut out of the center, placed in a pot.
*   **Theory:** Allows controlled steam escape (parachute effect) for slow reduction while maintaining 100% humidity over the food.
*   **Verdict:** No significant benefit over a standard lid for evaporation control or preventing surface drying.

### Enzymatic Peeling
*   **Enzymes:** Pectinex Ultra SPL and Pectinex Smash XXL.
*   **Optimization:** Pectinex Smash XXL is no longer necessary.
*   **Ratio:** Use slightly more Pectinex Ultra SPL to achieve the same result as the combination.
*   **Standard Dose:** ~4g per Liter of solution.

### Gelatin Noodles (Meat Glue Application)
*   **Technique:** Mix transglutaminase (meat glue) with gelatin.
*   **Properties:**
    *   Cross-linking prevents melting at body temperature and deep-frying temperatures.
    *   Texture: Breaks up nicely like pasta.
    *   Flavor Release: Good, though slightly less than pure gelatin due to cross-linking preventing complete melt. Superior to alginate (which traps flavor).
    *   Performance: Fries better than other hydrocolloid noodles.

### Low-Temperature Reductions (Rotovap Alternative)
*   **Equipment:** DIY aspirator setup (Erlenmeyer flask, sidearm, stir plate, ice bath).
*   **Advantage:** Can handle high vapor loads (unlike chamber vacuums/Gastrovac) without clogging.
*   **Application:** Reducing citrus juices, delicate aromas, sweet items (vacuum frying pineapple/apple chips) to prevent caramelization.

# Equipment Discussed

### Aspirator Pumps
*   **Type:** Recirculating aspirator pumps.
*   **Cost:** Used units ~$500; new units ~$1000.
*   **DIY Alternative:** Home Depot pump (~$80-$90) + PVC/aspirating nozzles.
*   **Tradeoff:** High water usage (gallons) vs. ability to handle heavy vapor loads.

### Stirring Hot Plates
*   **Source:** eBay (used).
*   **Cost:** ~$100.
*   **Characteristics:** Slow heat-up time (30 mins to stable temp), high stability, low wattage dump.
*   **Comparison:** Similar to French tops (spatial cooking, stable temp) but less responsive.

### Gastrovac
*   **Verdict:** Useless.
*   **Reason:** Uses a bad vacuum pump that cannot handle vapor loads required for boiling under vacuum.
*   **Better Alternative:** Chamber vacuum machine or DIY aspirator with ice bath.

### Modernist Pantry Ingredients
*   **Pectinex Ultra SPL:** Enzyme for peeling (4g/L).
*   **Pectinex Smash XXL:** Enzyme for peeling (optional, can be replaced by more Ultra SPL).
*   **Meat Glue (Transglutaminase):** For cross-linking gels.
*   **Hydrocolloids:** Various types (alginate, gelatin).
*   **pH Modifiers.**

### French Top
*   **Usage:** Spatial cooking (temperature varies by position on the burner).
*   **Benefit:** High stability, low responsiveness.

# Ingredients & Functional Roles

*   **Gelatin:**
    *   **Role:** Flavor release (melts at body temp, leaks moisture).
    *   **Tradeoff:** Cross-linking with meat glue prevents melting at body temp, slightly reducing flavor release compared to pure gelatin.
*   **Alginate:**
    *   **Role:** Gelling.
    *   **Tradeoff:** "Flavor thief"; traps moisture/flavor, poor flavor release unless used in very thin/soft shells or with high flavor concentration.
*   **Pectinex Ultra SPL:** Enzyme for breaking down pectin in citrus peels.
*   **Formaldehyde/Hydrogen Peroxide:** Historically used in Africa to extend milk shelf life; now considered unsafe/undesirable compared to hot holding.
*   **Bluefish/Mackerel:** Oily fish; prone to rapid texture/flavor degradation; benefits from ikajime.

# Strong Opinions & Recommendations

*   **Ikejime:** Definitely improves texture and flavor of line-caught fish. The stress of fighting the line is unavoidable, but ikajime mitigates post-catch stress.
*   **Bluefish:** Specifically recommended for testing ikajime due to rapid spoilage and texture issues.
*   **Molecular Gastronomy Label:** Hate the term "Molecular Gastronomy" and the "parlor tricks" (foams, gels) that accompany it when the food isn't fundamentally good.
*   **Canning:**
    *   Boiling water bath for 10 minutes is **not** sterilization; it is pasteurization.
    *   True sterilization requires pressure canning OR sufficient acidity/salt to prevent spore growth.
    *   Uncovered pickled green beans in a jar are a potential botulism risk.
*   **Cartouches:** Highly suspect that they make a significant difference compared to a lid; likely unnecessary.
*   **Gastrovac:** Useless technology; do not buy.
*   **Milk in Africa:** Hot holding is superior to cold chain logistics for smallholder farmers; matches consumer preference for boiled milk flavor.
*   **Candy Flavors:** Artificial cherry is a "vile abomination"; Popcorn jelly beans are the worst flavor ever.
*   **Egg Peeling:** Blowtorch method is highly effective for easy peeling.
*   **Equipment Access:** You do not need thousands of dollars in equipment to understand modern cooking; seeing what others do with toys is educational even if you cook with a stick.
