# Techniques

### Granita Texture Control
*   **Goal:** Achieve large, grainy crystals (distinct from smooth sorbet).
*   **Method:** Slow freezing with periodic stirring to break up and fluff forming crystals.
*   **Equipment Alternatives:**
    *   **Champion Juicer:** Freeze fruit/syrup mix rock hard. Attach a brine plate. Run frozen fruit through the juicer to create a passable sorbet/granita texture.
    *   **Blending Method:** Blend frozen cubes, then return to the fridge to harden slightly before serving.
    *   **Ice Cream Machine/Paco Jet:** These produce smooth textures; they are unsuitable for authentic granita unless the user accepts a non-granita result.
*   **Needle Ice Cream:** An old French technique involving milk/cream with very long crystals, achieved via slow freezing and periodic stirring.

### Sous Vide Egg Cooking (Fast Cook Method)
*   **Standard Method:** Cook eggs in a water bath at 62°C for 1 hour to ensure the yolk core reaches bath temperature.
*   **Fast Cook Method (High Temp/Short Time):**
    *   **Temperature:** 65°C, 70°C, or 77°C water bath.
    *   **Time:** Approximately 18 minutes.
    *   **Result:** Runny yolk texture with a firmer white.
*   **Batch Size Constraint:** Immersion circulators (1000W) struggle to recover temperature when dropping large batches (e.g., 100 eggs) into the bath. This technique is best for small batches (e.g., 4 eggs) where variables can be controlled consistently.
*   **Alternative Restaurant Method:** Boiling water (screaming boil) for a traditional overcooked white with a creamy center.

### Marmalade Preparation
*   **Peel Treatment:**
    1.  Score and peel citrus carefully.
    2.  Soak peels in a solution of **Pectinex SPL** and water (Ratio: 4g per Liter).
    3.  Scrub peels with a toothbrush/scrubby to remove the bitter albedo (white pith).
    4.  Sliver peels to desired size (larger slivers possible due to lack of pith).
    5.  Blanch if necessary (though bitterness is largely removed).
    6.  Cook marmalade base separately and fold in peels.
*   **Pectin Consideration:** Soaking removes pectin from the peel. The pulp usually contains enough pectin, but additional box pectin (citrus or apple derived) can be added if needed.

### Electroflotation (Liquid Clarification)
*   **Concept:** Use electrolysis to generate tiny hydrogen bubbles that float up, carrying impurities to the surface.
*   **Setup:** Carbon (graphite) rods and stainless steel top electrodes.
*   **Bubble Size:** Requires very fine wires to prevent immediate coalescence of bubbles.
*   **DIY Feasibility:** Aquarium pumps create bubbles that are too large; the mechanism relies on bubble generation from the wire surface itself, not a porous medium.

### Buffalo Chicken Milkshake (Deconstruction)
*   **Ingredients:** Milk, cream, blue cheese, tomato sauce, buffalo sauce, Worcestershire sauce.
*   **Process:** Blend ingredients, freeze in a pan stirring occasionally (like granita), then blend again.
*   **Critical Errors Identified:**
    *   **Texture:** Lack of sugar results in a grainy, gloppy texture rather than a milkshake consistency.
    *   **Correction:** Add **glucose syrup** (provides texture modification without sweetness).
    *   **Vegetarian Status:** Authentic Worcestershire sauce contains anchovies. It is not vegetarian. Vegetarian versions exist but are not recommended.

### Cheese Sauce Stabilization (Nathan Myhrvold Recipe)
*   **Ingredients:** Sodium citrate, iota carrageenan, cheese water, beer.
*   **Sodium Citrate (Melting Salt):** Solubilizes and disperses proteins, hydrates/swells proteins, and emulsifies fat. Prevents graininess in molten cheese.
*   **Iota Carrageenan:** Provides elasticity and moisture-holding ability, specifically for restructuring very mature/aged cheeses which have brittle textures due to protein breakdown.
*   **Alternative:** Use younger cheeses (3–6 months old) and omit iota carrageenan.

### Fish Killing (Ikajime)
*   **Problem:** Strong fighting fish (e.g., Bluefish) enter a hard rigor mortis that tears muscle structure, causing mushiness.
*   **Solution:** Spinal cord destruction (Ikajime) using a needle.
*   **Mechanism:** Stops nerve signals to muscles, preserving ATP. This delays rigor mortis, resulting in firmer flesh.

### Butternut Squash Skin Reaction
*   **Symptoms:** Dried-out skin, liquid pearls collecting on the surface.
*   **Cause:** Likely protein contact dermatitis or a hydrocolloid/protein mixture exuding from the fruit.
*   **Comparison:** Similar to reactions from handling shrimp.
*   **Status:** No definitive scientific consensus found; suspected to be a specific squash/melon family defense mechanism.

# Equipment

### Thermocouple Thermometers
*   **Characteristics:** Not extraordinarily accurate out of the box; require calibration.
*   **Probe Types:**
    *   **Hypermic Probes:** For sous vide (penetrate bags). Price range: $40–$80+.
    *   **Custom Probes:** Can be made by ordering thermocouple wire from McMaster-Carr.
*   **Data Acquisition:** Measurement Computing DAQ unit (~$150) allows 8-channel thermocouple input into a computer.

### Immersion Blenders
*   **Status:** Dave Arnold expresses dissatisfaction with current market options.
*   **Future Project:** One of the first items to be developed for his new company, "Bub."

### Pressure Cookers
*   **Recommendation:** **Cuisinart** (specifically non-venting models).
*   **Reasoning:** High-quality pans, durable (can withstand continuous abuse), non-venting preserves flavor better than venting models.
*   **Venting Models:** Reduce flavor quality (good for stocks, bad for others).
*   **Electric Models:** Quisnaut (brand name mentioned) requires modification to reach standard 15 PSI (temperature too low out of the box). *Note: Dave advises against modification due to safety risks.*
*   **Large Capacity:** Giant American pressure canners are too large for home use.

### Cream Whippers
*   **Recommendation:** **ISI**.
*   **Reasoning:** Off-brand/cheap whippers often fail to seal and leak. ISI is worth the extra cost for reliability.

### Vacuum Sealers
*   **Chamber Vacuum Sealers:**
    *   **Cost Drivers:** Stainless steel construction and high-quality vacuum pumps (Busch, Germany).
    *   **Pump Durability:** Busch pumps withstand contamination and vapor boiling well ("Tonka trucks").
    *   **Price:** Expensive ($1,500–$22,000).
*   **FoodSaver:** Weak vacuum; not suitable for vacuum infusion. Good for individual bagging to prevent freezer burn.
*   **Ziploc (SC Johnson):** ~$6. Weak vacuum. Not suitable for infusion. Good for bagging muffins.
*   **DIY Alternative:** Water displacement method using Ziploc bags.

### Digital Scales
*   **Requirements:**
    *   Hydrocolloid work: Measure to 0.1g.
    *   Meat work: Measure many kilos.
*   **Recommendation:** **A&D Washdown Scale**.
    *   **Features:** Waterproof (dishwasher safe), large capacity, stays on when plugged in (prevents re-weighing if interrupted).
*   **Avoid:** Scales that turn off automatically.
*   **High-End Alternative:** Barra Viva (12kg capacity, 0.1g precision, counting scale) – very slow, expensive to replicate today.

### Sauce Funnels (Sauce Guns)
*   **Standard:** Metal funnel with a central stick to control aperture.
*   **Retailer:** JB Prince (carries high-quality brands, higher price, not high margin).
*   **Alternatives:** Amazon options (~50% cheaper) often have poor reviews (don't work).
*   **DIY:** Possible but not cost-effective for ~$150 price point.

### Circulators
*   **Brand:** PolyScience (mentioned by caller Christoph).
*   **Application:** Precise temperature control for texture development (e.g., eggs).

# Ingredients & Functional Roles

*   **Pectinex SPL:** Enzyme used to remove bitter albedo (white pith) from citrus peels.
*   **Glucose Syrup:** Texture modifier for ice creams/sorbets; mimics sugar's textural properties without adding sweetness. Essential for savory milkshakes to prevent graininess.
*   **Sodium Citrate:** Melting salt. Solubilizes/disperses proteins, hydrates/swells proteins, emulsifies fat. Stabilizes molten cheese emulsions.
*   **Iota Carrageenan:** Hydrocolloid. Provides elasticity and moisture holding. Essential for using mature/aged cheeses in sauces.
*   **Lactic Acid:** Flavor component found in cheese, cured meats, pickles, sauerkraut, kimchi. Can be added to mimic these flavors.
*   **Malic Acid + Citric Acid (2:1 Ratio):** Mimics the acidity profile of lime juice. Used as an acidity corrector.
*   **Ascorbic Acid (Vitamin C):** Antioxidant. Prevents oxidation in juices.
*   **Anchovies:** Primary ingredient in authentic Worcestershire sauce; makes it non-vegetarian.
