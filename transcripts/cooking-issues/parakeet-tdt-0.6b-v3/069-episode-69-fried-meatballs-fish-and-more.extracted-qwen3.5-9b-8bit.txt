# Techniques & Parameters

### Meatball Preparation (Sous Vide / Low-Temp)
*   **Diameter:** ~3.5 cm (1.5 inches).
*   **Pre-cooking Step:** Flash fry meatballs in hot oil for ~30 seconds before bagging. Do not let them sit on a tray after frying as they will break under their own weight.
*   **Bagging:** Place flash-fried meatballs directly into a bag. Recommended to use butter in the bag; avoid sauce if planning to flash fry afterwards.
*   **Cooking Temperature:** 55°C to 57°C (131°F to 135°F).
    *   *Note:* 56°C yields a rare/medium-rare interior. 57°C is preferred by the host.
*   **Cooking Time:** ~30 minutes minimum.
    *   *Tenderizing:* Cooking for several hours at 55°C–56°C further tenderizes ground meat.
*   **Finishing:** Pan-fry at 350°F (177°C) for ~2 minutes after sous vide.
*   **Texture Control:**
    *   *Firm/Sausage-like:* Increase salt, increase kneading, and extend cooking time.
    *   *Loose/Hamburger-like:* Add more wet ingredients/fillers, decrease salt, decrease kneading.
    *   *Mixing Timing:* Mix meatball ingredients, form, and bag immediately. Do not let the mix sit before forming.

### Pork Shoulder (Sous Vide)
*   **Temperature:** 58°C (136°F).
*   **Time:** ~34 hours.
*   **Prep:** Brine in basic salt/sugar solution for 12 hours.
*   **Searing:** Cast iron grill pan or grill.
*   **Color Note:** Long cooking times at 58°C result in a "rosy" rather than bright pink center. Brining enhances this color change.

### Pork Shanks (Sous Vide)
*   **Temperature:** 62°C to 63°C (144°F to 145°F).
    *   *Warning:* 65°C causes excessive moisture loss and muscle contraction.
*   **Time:** 34 to 48 hours.
*   **Texture Goal:** Super tender but not falling apart (collagen softens to gelatin but retains structure).
*   **Batch Size Warning:** Do not max out circulator capacity (e.g., 30L) with 30 shanks.
    *   *Risk:* Center of bags may not reach temp quickly, allowing lacto-lactic acid bacteria growth (puffed bags, bad smell).
    *   *Remedy 1:* Do not pack bags too tightly.
    *   *Remedy 2:* Blanch bags in simmering water for 35–45 seconds (while meat is cold) to kill surface bacteria before circulating.
*   **Salting Strategy:**
    *   *For "fresh pork" taste:* Do not salt or brine before sous vide. Add salt and aromatics only after frying and before bagging with sauce.
    *   *For Brined flavor:* Brine beforehand.
    *   *Sauce in Bag:* If cooking with sauce in the bag, hyper-reduce the sauce first; otherwise, meat juices will dilute it to a poached flavor.

### Deep Frying & Holding
*   **Timing:** Fry ahead of service to avoid smoke in the venue.
*   **Holding:** Do not hold on racks (crust gets soggy).
*   **Recommended Method:**
    1.  Remove from bag.
    2.  Quick sear to develop crust.
    3.  Place directly into warm sauce in a ziplock bag.
    4.  Keep warm in a circulator bath.
    *   *Rationale:* Sauce integration is more important than crust retention for braised items.

### Glucose Syrup
*   **Composition:** Water, glucose, maltose, longer chain maltodextrins.
*   **DE (Dextrose Equivalent):** Percentage of reducing sugars (glucose/maltose) relative to total solids.
    *   *Relationship:* Lower DE = less sweet, less broken down starch. Higher DE = sweeter, more broken down.
    *   *No Direct Sweetness Correlation:* DE does not directly predict sweetness due to varying sugar compositions.
*   **Common Types:**
    *   **DE20:** Used for ice cream texture without excessive sweetness.
    *   **DE40:** Higher sweetness/breakdown.
*   **Homemade Difficulty:** Extremely difficult to replicate commercial syrups from powders (dextrose, maltose, maltodextrins) due to required heat and specific temperature control to achieve correct viscosity and solids content (~80%+ solids).
*   **Recommendation:** Buy from suppliers (e.g., Modernist Pantry) rather than attempting DIY.

### Fish Allergies (Scale vs. Skin)
*   **Cause:** Allergy to parvalbumin proteins, not scales.
*   **Target Group:** Teleost fish (fish with ray scales).
*   **Safe Groups:** Non-teleost fish (e.g., eels, lampreys, sharks, cartilaginous fish).
*   **Evolutionary Rule:** Allergy risk increases with taxonomic closeness to the allergen source.

# Ingredients & Functional Roles

*   **Glucose Syrup:**
    *   *Function:* Texture modification (e.g., ice cream), fermentation prevention, viscosity control.
    *   *Property:* Not very sweet despite the name.
*   **Salt:**
    *   *Function in Meatballs:* Increases firmness (protein extraction), enhances flavor. Trade-off between texture (firm vs. loose) and flavor.
*   **Brine (Salt/Sugar):**
    *   *Function:* Flavor infusion, texture firming, color modification (reduces bright pinkness in pork).
*   **Parvalbumin:**
    *   *Function:* Major allergen in fin fish (95% of fin fish allergies).
*   **Lulo:**
    *   *Context:* Fruit from Colombia. Frozen version available in NY but inferior to fresh.
