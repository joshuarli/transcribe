# Equipment Recommendations & Tradeoffs

### Immersion Circulators
*   **Recommendation:** Purchase a second unit immediately.
*   **Rationale:** Allows one unit to run while the other is used for service; prevents regret.
*   **Cost:** Approximately $800 per unit.
*   **Availability:** Available at J.B. Prince and Williams-Sonoma.
*   **DIY Circulators:**
    *   **Feasibility:** Possible, but often results in less functional equipment due to time spent building.
    *   **Key Components:**
        *   **Controller:** $3–$5 (available on eBay or Aubur Instruments).
        *   **Relay:** Avoid standard relays (failure point); use Solid State Relays (SSR).
        *   **Heater:** Immersion heater (can be sourced from hot water heaters).
        *   **Sensor:** Temperature sensor (several dollars).
        *   **Pump:** The most difficult part to source; commercial pumps have long shafts with stainless steel blades for longevity. Plastic pumps (e.g., InSinkErator style) fail over time.
    *   **Case:** Can be laser-cut (e.g., Pinoco.com) or custom-built.

### Dehydrators
*   **Top Recommendation:** Excalibur.
*   **Reasons:**
    *   Square racks allow logical layout of items (unlike round racks with holes in the middle).
    *   Even drying throughout (top and bottom racks perform equally).
    *   Suitable for flat sheets, liquids (leathers/papers), and fermentation experiments.
    *   Restaurant-grade reliability; other brands often regretted in professional settings.
*   **Alternative:** Round models are inferior for layout efficiency.

### Meat Slicers
*   **Top Recommendation:** Hobart Model 3000.
    *   **Features:** Vertical slicing mechanism (product sits flat; carriage moves product into blade).
    *   **Maintenance:** Blade pops out for dishwasher cleaning; props up like a car for cleaning underneath.
    *   **Performance:** Slices perfectly without pressure; blade is extremely sharp.
*   **Previous Recommendation:** Berserba SE12.
    *   **Status:** Still excellent, cheaper than Hobart 3000.
    *   **Tradeoff:** Does not clean as easily; gravity-fed mechanism requires more pressure.
*   **Gravity Slicers (Deli Standard):**
    *   **Mechanism:** Product on an angle; gravity forces product into blade.
    *   **Risk:** Requires significant pressure to slice, causing the bottom of the meat to wedge out ("healing out"). Indicates a dull blade, poor slicer, or excessive user pressure.
    *   **Ideal Use:** Only works well if the blade is perfectly sharp and the machine is high quality.

### Vacuum Sealers
*   **Selection Criteria:**
    *   **Chamber Size:** Prioritize large chamber size (e.g., MiniPacks) over brand programming features.
    *   **Pump:** Must have a German-made, oil-based vacuum pump (brand name starts with 'B').
    *   **Avoid:** Cheap units with inferior pumps.

### Smokers
*   **Recommendation:** Buy cheap.
*   **Specific Model:** "Little Chief" or "Big Chief" (quilted metal box with bottom heater).
*   **Modification:** Drill hole in side, attach smoke generator with heater to cool smoke.
*   **Rationale:** Light, easy to move/stow, cheap, and widely used in NYC restaurants (e.g., WD-50, Som).

### Carbonation Systems (Bar Use)
*   **Top Recommendation:** Soda bottles with carbonator caps (Liquid Bread Corporation) connected to 5 or 20 lb CO2 tanks.
*   **Process:**
    *   Pre-chill bottles until almost frozen.
    *   Carbonate 3 times before service.
    *   Use 1L bottles (holds ~5 pours).
*   **Avoid:**
    *   **ISI Soda Bottles:** High cost of chargers; creates excessive foam; inconsistent carbonation levels.
    *   **Keg Systems:** Cause excessive foaming.
*   **Refrigeration:** Use a specialized refrigerator (e.g., Randall FX) set to 22–23°F for carbonated drinks.

### Puffing Guns (Cereal/Rice Puffing)
*   **Concept:** Seals grain (wheat, rice) in a container, heats under massive pressure (150–175 psi), then rapidly depressurizes to expand.
*   **Temperature:** ~350–360°F.
*   **Moisture Content:** Grain must have ~10% moisture.
*   **Commercial Options:**
    *   **Puritan Manufacturing Corporation:** Old puffing guns refurbished and used in Mexico; moved to continuous puffers.
    *   **Twin Screw Extruder:** Used for Captain Crunch; invented for plastics industry; uses screw under enormous pressure to heat and extrude grain.
    *   **Korean Machines:** Small commercial machines for puffed rice cakes (e.g., *Bing*).
*   **DIY Warning:** Street vendors use homemade boilers with open fires and vice grips; extremely dangerous (150+ psi). DIY attempts with pipes (Schedule 80) often fail due to sealing issues at high temps/pressures.

# Ingredients & Functional Roles

### Lecithin
*   **Usage:** Attempted for creating juice foam.
*   **Verdict:** Not recommended. Difficult to mix with juice; sticks to equipment (e.g., spring coil frothers); unpleasant to work with ("like roofing tar").

### VersaWhip
*   **Function:** Whipping agent.
*   **Recipe Ratio:** 200g Orange Juice + 2.5g VersaWhip.
*   **Note:** Requires Xanthan gum (0.5–1% range) to provide body and hold the foam after whipping.
*   **Application:** Used in dense citrus foams (e.g., blood orange foam).

### Agar
*   **Function:** Gelation agent to stabilize foams.
*   **Recipe Ratio:** 0.8% to 1% agar gel.
*   **Process:** Mix juice + acid (lemon) + agar into a fluid gel. Blend with cream in an ISI whipper. Result: Dense, creamy foam that holds structure.

### Alliums (Onions, Garlic, Ramps)
*   **Pressure Cooking:**
    *   **Application:** Making massive amounts of sauce (e.g., half-garlic pizza sauce) or stock (e.g., beef stock, veg stock).
    *   **Flavor Profile:** Incredibly sweet, but potentially light on "onion flavor" if used alone.
*   **Varieties:** Different garlic varieties are useful as the season progresses; varieties climatize to the garden over 2–3 years.
*   **Ramps:**
    *   **Opinion:** The product is good, but the popularity is annoying.
    *   **Season:** Ending; Green garlic and chives season beginning.

# Techniques & Parameters

### Carbonated Drink Ratios
*   **Standard Ratio (5 oz pour):**
    *   1.75 oz Strong Liquor
    *   3.5 oz Water (includes simple syrup, juice, etc.)
*   **Alcohol Content Warning:**
    *   Drinks with >32–33% alcohol require extremely low temperatures (~-18°C / 0°F) to carbonate properly.
    *   High-alcohol carbonated drinks foam excessively, taste cloying over time, and can be physically overwhelming ("hit people like a ton of bricks").
*   **Preparation:**
    *   **Shaking:** Required for carbonated drinks (stirring is insufficient for temperature and dilution).
    *   **Temperature:** Chill until almost frozen (22–23°F recommended).
    *   **Dilution:** Add soda water to high-alcohol carbonated drinks to improve balance and refreshment.

### Foam Creation (Citrus)
*   **Method 1 (VersaWhip + Xanthan):**
    1.  Mix 200g juice + 2.5g VersaWhip.
    2.  Add Xanthan gum for stability.
    3.  Whip with immersion blender (whisk attachment).
    4.  Apply to dish.
*   **Method 2 (Agar + Cream):**
    1.  Acidify juice (e.g., add lemon to orange).
    2.  Set into 0.8–1% agar gel.
    3.  Blend into fluid gel.
    4.  Mix with straight cream in ISI whipper.
    5.  Squirt to create dense, stable foam.

### Puffing Grain (Theoretical)
*   **Process:**
    1.  Select grain with ~10% moisture.
    2.  Seal in container.
    3.  Heat under 150–175 psi to ~350–360°F.
    4.  Rapidly release pressure to cause explosive expansion.
*   **Challenge:** Requires robust sealing and external heat agitation (rotation) to ensure even heating; internal heating with water medium often causes venting/steam leaks.
