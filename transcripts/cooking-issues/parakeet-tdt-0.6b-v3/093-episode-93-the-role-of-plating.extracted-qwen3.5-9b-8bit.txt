# Techniques & Parameters

*   **Shrimp Boil (Correction):** Do not cook shrimp, sausage, corn, onion, potato, garlic, and zatarain's spice mix together sous vide. Cook all components separately to perfection, then recombine at the end.
*   **Lobster Steaming (High Volume):** Can be achieved using a "steam giant" (industrial steam cleaner) capable of processing 25,000 lobsters in 5 days.
*   **Juice Clarification (Centrifuge):**
    *   **Enzyme:** Use Pectanex Ultra SPL (available at modernistPantry.com).
    *   **Speed:** High-speed centrifuges (e.g., Modest Cuisine) can reach 48,000g. Lower speed units (e.g., 1,300g) work but are slower and less effective.
    *   **Volume:** Small portable units process ~120ml (4oz) per spin.
*   **Vermouth Preservation (Oxidation Prevention):**
    *   **Storage Temp:** Store below freezing but above the liquid's freezing point.
    *   **Purging Method:** Drop liquid nitrogen into the bottle, cap immediately while vapor is actively pushing air out.
    *   **Alternative Gases:** Use Argon (molecular weight ~39.9, heavier than air) or Carbon Dioxide (molecular weight ~44, heavier than air) to purge headspace. Nitrogen (molecular weight ~28) is slightly lighter than air and may float out.
    *   **Stability:** Diluted vermouth in bottles purged with Argon or CO2 and capped is "dead stable" under refrigeration.
*   **Cherry Preservation (Shelf-Stable Maraschino/Bourbon Style):**
    *   **Liquor:** Must use high-proof ethanol.
    *   **Cherry Type:** Sour cherries (e.g., Morello) are preferred; sweet cherries result in insipid flavor.
    *   **Stem:** Do not remove the stem; removing it causes the fruit to turn mushy.
    *   **Enzymatic Treatment (Optional):** Soak in Pectin Methyl Esterase (brand: NovoShape from Novozymes) and a small amount of calcium to firm up cell walls.
    *   **Calcium Addition:** Adding a tiny bit of calcium to the high-proof ethanol helps crosslink pectin to prevent breakdown.
*   **Soba Noodle Making:** Ongoing work to solve issues with soba preparation (mentioned as a current project).

# Equipment: Names, Models, Tradeoffs, Recommendations

*   **Centrifuges:**
    *   **Booker & Dax / FCI Unit:** 3L benchtop, ~4,000g force. Described as the "sweet spot" for professional use.
    *   **Modest Cuisine Unit:** Capable of 48,000g. Too large and expensive for home use.
    *   **Portable Unit (Dave's Columbia Test):** ~$200, 1,300g force, 120ml capacity. Good for home testing/drinks, insufficient for bars.
    *   **Safety:** Never use Mason jars. Use specific centrifuge bottles or tough plastic bottles designed for rotors. Must balance perfectly before spinning.
    *   **Maintenance:** Bleach thoroughly, pressure cook rotors to kill biological contaminants, avoid detergents on aluminum buckets.
*   **Grills:**
    *   **Weber Kettle:** Inexpensive, good for radiating heat, but lacks effective coal height adjustment.
    *   **Egg Grill:** Expensive (~$560), fuel-efficient, but Dave prefers larger grills for versatility.
    *   **Ideal Features:** Heavy cast iron grates (absorb energy better than thin wire), large surface area for high/low zoning, adjustable grate height.
    *   **Gas Assist:** Recommended for convenience. Use lava rocks on gas burners to simulate charcoal flavor (fat dripping/vaporizing) if burners lack sufficient "oomph."
*   **Immersion Blenders:**
    *   **BAMIX:** Recommended for $99. Features a 4-prong open blade guard reducing cavitation in thick products (e.g., pancake batter). Shaft does not pop off for cleaning (downside).
    *   **KitchenAid:** More expensive, comes with useless attachments (whisk/chopper).
    *   **Commercial Option:** RoboKou (bell pops off to clean, but cleaning underneath the blade is still a hassle).
    *   **General Issues:** Splashing in improperly sized cups; cavitation around stamped steel bells in thick products.
*   **Tablet Press:**
    *   **Riva Mini Press:** Benchtop unit, size of a floor-standing Apple Mac. ~$10,000 new, cheaper via pharmaceutical auctions. Single station, versatile, requires only one set of tooling.
*   **Vermouth Storage:**
    *   **VacuVin Stopper:** Ineffective for long-term oxidation prevention in vermouth.
    *   **Bottle Capper:** Essential for sealing after gas purging. Note: Carpano Antica bottles may not accept standard champagne-style bottle caps.
*   **Moving Equipment:**
    *   **Shrink Wrap:** Recommended for protecting equipment during moves to prevent truck scratches.

# Ingredients & Functional Roles

*   **Pectanex Ultra SPL:** Enzyme used for juice clarification in centrifuge processes.
*   **Pectin Methyl Esterase (NovoShape):** Enzyme that firms up fruit cell walls, allowing boiling without breaking (used for raspberries).
*   **Calcium:** Crosslinks pectin to firm up fruit; added to high-proof ethanol for cherry preservation.
*   **Argon:** Inert gas (MW ~39.9), heavier than air, used to purge oxygen from vermouth bottles.
*   **Carbon Dioxide:** Gas (MW ~44), heavier than air, can purge oxygen but may leave a "pringly" sensation.
*   **Nitrogen:** Gas (MW ~28), slightly lighter than air, floats out of bottles; not ideal for long-term storage purging.
*   **Surimi:** Imitation crab product made from bleached fish paste. Texture can be a barrier for picky eaters.
*   **Corn-Based Pasta:** Acceptable alternative to traditional wheat pasta; better than rice pasta but lacks the "pasta" texture.
*   **Kangaroo Meat:** Suggested as a novel protein source to encourage picky eaters (leveraging the "cute animal" connection).

**Caller: Rob Trepaz**
*   **Question:** Carpano Antica Formula vermouth oxidizes quickly. How to rebottle/preserve it? Should other low-proof liquors (Lillet, Cointreau, Dubonnet, Aperol) be stored similarly?
*   **Answer:** Vermouth oxidizes easily. Do not use CO2 alone (may cause tingling). Best method: Purge headspace with Argon or CO2, then cap immediately. Store below freezing (but above liquid freezing point). Other low-proof spirits (Lillet, Cointreau, Dubonnet) should be treated similarly; Carpano is the most critical. Aperol is less concerning.
